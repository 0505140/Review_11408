## 一、中英文主旨
**中文主旨**：本文系统介绍了在演讲中有效运用幽默的五个核心方法，强调幽默必须紧密贴合听众的共同经历与立场，通过针对性的素材选择、自然流畅的表达以及对语言技巧的灵活运用，才能真正让听众产生共鸣并会心一笑。
**英文主旨**：This passage systematically introduces five core methods for effectively using humor in speeches. It emphasizes that humor must be closely aligned with the audience's shared experiences and perspectives, and can truly resonate with the audience and make them smile through targeted material selection, natural expression and flexible use of language skills.

## 二、逐句翻译与解析
### 第一段
1. **原文**：If you intend using humor in your talk to make people smile, you must know how to identify shared experiences and problems.
   **翻译**：如果你想在演讲中运用幽默来逗人发笑，你必须知道如何识别听众的共同经历和共同问题。
   **常考词汇短语**：
   - intend doing sth 打算/想要做某事
   - identify v. 识别，认出，确定
   - shared adj. 共享的，共同的
   - experience n. 经历（可数）；经验（不可数）
   **句子结构**：条件状语从句+主句结构。`If you intend...`为条件状语从句，不定式`to make people smile`作目的状语，说明运用幽默的目的。

2. **原文**：Your humor must be relevant to the audience and should help to show them that you are one of them or that you understand their situation and are in sympathy with their point of view.
   **翻译**：你的幽默必须与听众相关，并且应该有助于向他们表明你是他们中的一员，或者你理解他们的处境并赞同他们的观点。
   **常考词汇短语**：
   - be relevant to 与……相关
   - audience n. 观众，听众
   - situation n. 情况，处境
   - be in sympathy with 赞同，同情
   - point of view 观点，看法
   **长难句分析**：并列复合句。主句主语为`Your humor`，并列谓语为`must be relevant to...`和`should help to show...`。`show`后接两个由`that`引导的并列宾语从句：`that you are one of them`和`that you understand their situation and are in sympathy with their point of view`。第二个宾语从句中又包含`understand`和`are in sympathy with`两个并列谓语。

3. **原文**：Depending on whom you are addressing, the problems will be different.
   **翻译**：根据你演讲的对象不同，你们的共同问题也会有所不同。
   **常考词汇短语**：
   - depend on 取决于，依靠
   - address v. 向……讲话，对……发表演说；处理，解决
   **句子结构**：现在分词短语`Depending on whom you are addressing`作条件状语，其中`whom`引导宾语从句作介词`on`的宾语。

4. **原文**：If you are talking to a group of managers, you may refer to the disorganized methods of their secretaries; alternatively if you are addressing secretaries, you may want to comment on their disorganized bosses.
   **翻译**：如果你在和一群经理讲话，你可以提及他们秘书杂乱无章的工作方式；反过来，如果你在对秘书们发表演说，你或许可以评论一下他们那些做事毫无条理的老板。
   **常考词汇短语**：
   - refer to 提及，谈到；参考
   - disorganized adj. 杂乱无章的，缺乏条理的
   - secretary n. 秘书
   - alternatively adv. 或者，反过来
   - comment on 评论，谈论
   **句子结构**：分号连接两个并列的条件状语从句结构，每个分句均为`if引导的条件状语从句+主句`。

**本段概括**：提出运用幽默的首要核心原则：幽默必须基于听众的共同经历和问题，且要根据不同的演讲对象灵活调整幽默内容，确保与听众立场一致。

### 第二段
1. **原文**：Here is an example, which I heard at a nurses' convention, of a story which works well because the audience all shared the same view of doctors.
   **翻译**：这里有一个我在护士大会上听到的例子，这个故事之所以效果很好，是因为所有听众对医生都有着相同的看法。
   **常考词汇短语**：
   - convention n. 大会，会议；惯例，习俗
   - work well 效果好，运行良好
   - share the same view of 对……持有相同看法
   **长难句分析**：多重复合句。主句为`Here is an example`。第一个`which`引导非限制性定语从句`which I heard at a nurses' convention`修饰`example`；介词短语`of a story`作后置定语修饰`example`；第二个`which`引导限制性定语从句`which works well`修饰`story`；`because`引导原因状语从句说明故事效果好的原因。

2. **原文**：A man arrives in heaven and is being shown around by St. Peter.
   **翻译**：一个人来到了天堂，圣彼得正带着他四处参观。
   **常考词汇短语**：
   - arrive in 到达（大地点）
   - heaven n. 天堂
   - show sb around 带某人四处参观

3. **原文**：He sees wonderful accommodations, beautiful gardens, sunny weather, and so on.
   **翻译**：他看到了极好的住所、美丽的花园、晴朗的天气等等。
   **常考词汇短语**：
   - accommodation n. 住所，住宿（常用复数）
   - and so on 等等，诸如此类

4. **原文**：Everyone is very peaceful, polite and friendly until, waiting in a line for lunch, the new arrival is suddenly pushed aside by a man in a white coat, who rushes to the head of the line, grabs his food and stomps over to a table by himself.
   **翻译**：所有人都非常平和、礼貌且友善，直到在排队吃午饭的时候，这个新来的人突然被一个穿白大褂的男人推到一边，那人冲到队伍最前面，抓起自己的食物，噔噔噔地独自走到一张桌子旁。
   **常考词汇短语**：
   - peaceful adj. 平和的，和平的
   - polite adj. 有礼貌的
   - wait in a line 排队
   - arrival n. 到达者，到来
   - push aside 推开，推到一边
   - white coat 白大褂（代指医生）
   - rush to 冲向
   - grab v. 抓住，抢夺
   - stomp v. 重踩，跺脚走
   **长难句分析**：复合句。主句为`Everyone is very peaceful, polite and friendly`。`until`引导时间状语从句，从句主干为`the new arrival is suddenly pushed aside by a man in a white coat`；现在分词短语`waiting in a line for lunch`作时间状语说明背景；`who`引导非限制性定语从句修饰`a man in a white coat`，该从句包含`rushes`、`grabs`和`stomps`三个并列谓语动词。

5. **原文**：“Who is that?” the new arrival asked St. Peter.
   **翻译**：“那是谁？”新来的人问圣彼得。

6. **原文**：“Oh, that’s God,” came the reply, “but sometimes he thinks he’s a doctor.”
   **翻译**：“哦，那是上帝，”圣彼得回答说，“但有时候他觉得自己是个医生。”
   **常考词汇短语**：
   - reply n. & v. 回答，答复

**本段概括**：通过一个在护士大会上大获成功的笑话进行举例论证，直观说明当幽默内容精准契合听众对特定群体的共同看法时，就能产生极佳的共鸣效果。

### 第三段
1. **原文**：If you are part of the group which you are addressing, you will be in a position to know the experiences and problems which are common to all of you and it’ll be appropriate for you to make a passing remark about the inedible canteen food or the chairman’s notorious bad taste in ties.
   **翻译**：如果你是你所演讲的群体中的一员，你就能够了解你们所有人共有的经历和问题，那么你随口评论一下食堂难以下咽的饭菜或者主席那出了名的糟糕领带品味，都是合适的。
   **常考词汇短语**：
   - be in a position to do sth 能够做某事，有资格做某事
   - common to 为……所共有
   - appropriate adj. 合适的，恰当的
   - make a passing remark about 随口评论一下
   - inedible adj. 不能吃的，难以下咽的
   - canteen n. 食堂，餐厅
   - notorious adj. 声名狼藉的，臭名昭著的
   - taste n. 品味，味道
   - tie n. 领带
   **长难句分析**：并列复合句。`if`引导条件状语从句，从句中`which you are addressing`是定语从句修饰`the group`。主句由`and`连接两个并列分句：第一个分句`you will be in a position to know the experiences and problems`中，`which are common to all of you`是定语从句修饰`the experiences and problems`；第二个分句`it’ll be appropriate for you to make a passing remark about...`中，`it`是形式主语，真正主语是后面的不定式短语。

2. **原文**：With other audiences you mustn’t attempt to cut in with humor as they will resent an outsider making disparaging remarks about their canteen or their chairman.
   **翻译**：而对于其他听众，你千万不要试图插入这类幽默，因为他们会反感一个外人对他们的食堂或者主席发表贬低性的言论。
   **常考词汇短语**：
   - attempt to do sth 试图做某事
   - cut in 插嘴，插入
   - resent v. 怨恨，反感
   - outsider n. 外人，局外人
   - disparaging adj. 贬低的，诋毁的
   **句子结构**：`as`引导原因状语从句，说明不能对外部听众使用内部幽默的原因。

3. **原文**：You will be on safer ground if you stick to scapegoats like the Post Office or the telephone system.
   **翻译**：如果你拿邮局或电话系统这类“替罪羊”来开玩笑，就会安全得多。
   **常考词汇短语**：
   - be on safer ground 处于更安全的境地
   - stick to 坚持，固守
   - scapegoat n. 替罪羊
   - Post Office 邮局
   - telephone system 电话系统

**本段概括**：明确幽默素材的边界：只有作为听众群体的内部成员时，才能拿该群体内部的事物开玩笑；对于外部听众，应选择邮局、电话系统等无特定群体归属的公共“替罪羊”作为幽默素材。

### 第四段
1. **原文**：If you feel awkward being humorous, you must practice so that it becomes more natural.
   **翻译**：如果你觉得幽默让你感到尴尬，你必须多加练习，让幽默变得更加自然。
   **常考词汇短语**：
   - awkward adj. 尴尬的，笨拙的
   - so that 以便，为了
   - natural adj. 自然的

2. **原文**：Include a few casual and apparently off-the-cuff remarks which you can deliver in a relaxed and unforced manner.
   **翻译**：加入一些随意的、看似即兴的评论，你可以用一种轻松自然的方式把它们表达出来。
   **常考词汇短语**：
   - casual adj. 随意的，随便的
   - apparently adv. 显然地，表面上
   - off-the-cuff adj. 即兴的，未经准备的
   - deliver v. 发表（演讲），递送
   - relaxed adj. 放松的
   - unforced adj. 自然的，不勉强的
   - manner n. 方式，态度
   **句子结构**：`which`引导定语从句`which you can deliver in a relaxed and unforced manner`修饰`remarks`。

3. **原文**：Often it’s the delivery which causes the audience to smile, so speak slowly and remember that a raised eyebrow or an unbelieving look may help to show that you are making a light-hearted remark.
   **翻译**：很多时候，是表达方式让听众发笑，所以你要放慢语速，并且记住，抬一下眉毛或者露出一副难以置信的表情，都有助于表明你在说一句轻松的玩笑话。
   **常考词汇短语**：
   - delivery n. （演讲的）表达方式，递送
   - cause sb to do sth 导致某人做某事
   - raised eyebrow 挑眉
   - unbelieving adj. 难以置信的
   - light-hearted adj. 轻松的，无忧无虑的
   **长难句分析**：并列复合句。第一个分句是强调句`it’s the delivery which causes the audience to smile`，强调主语`the delivery`。`so`引导结果状语从句，从句包含两个并列祈使句`speak slowly`和`remember that...`。`remember`后接宾语从句，该从句中`show`又接一个宾语从句说明表明的内容。

**本段概括**：介绍了提升幽默表达自然度的具体方法：通过反复练习消除尴尬感，在演讲中加入即兴评论，同时注意控制语速并配合恰当的面部表情来强化幽默效果。

### 第五段
1. **原文**：Look for the humor.
   **翻译**：要善于发现幽默。

2. **原文**：It often comes from the unexpected.
   **翻译**：幽默往往来自于意料之外。
   **常考词汇短语**：
   - unexpected adj. 意外的，想不到的（`the+形容词`表示一类事物）

3. **原文**：A twist on a familiar quote “If at first you don’t succeed, give up” or a play on words or on a situation.
   **翻译**：可以对熟悉的名言进行改编（比如“如果一开始你没有成功，那就放弃吧”），或者玩文字游戏，或者利用情境制造幽默。
   **常考词汇短语**：
   - twist n. 扭转，改编，曲解
   - familiar adj. 熟悉的
   - quote n. 引语，名言
   - play on words 文字游戏，双关语

4. **原文**：Search for exaggeration and understatement.
   **翻译**：去寻找夸张和轻描淡写的表达手法。
   **常考词汇短语**：
   - search for 寻找
   - exaggeration n. 夸张，夸大
   - understatement n. 轻描淡写，低调陈述

5. **原文**：Look at your talk and pick out a few words or sentences which you can turn about and inject with humor.
   **翻译**：审视你的演讲稿，挑出几个你可以改写并注入幽默元素的单词或句子。
   **常考词汇短语**：
   - pick out 挑选出
   - turn about 改写，扭转
   - inject v. 注入，注射
   **句子结构**：`which`引导定语从句`which you can turn about and inject with humor`修饰`words or sentences`。

**本段概括**：指出幽默的四大常见来源：意料之外的转折、文字游戏、夸张手法和轻描淡写手法，并建议在演讲稿中主动挑选合适的词句进行幽默化改造。

## 三、完整原文
① If you intend using humor in your talk to make people smile, you must know how to identify shared experiences and problems. Your humor must be relevant to the audience and should help to show them that you are one of them or that you understand their situation and are in sympathy with their point of view. Depending on whom you are addressing, the problems will be different. If you are talking to a group of managers, you may refer to the disorganized methods of their secretaries; alternatively if you are addressing secretaries, you may want to comment on their disorganized bosses.

② Here is an example, which I heard at a nurses' convention, of a story which works well because the audience all shared the same view of doctors. A man arrives in heaven and is being shown around by St. Peter. He sees wonderful accommodations, beautiful gardens, sunny weather, and so on. Everyone is very peaceful, polite and friendly until, waiting in a line for lunch, the new arrival is suddenly pushed aside by a man in a white coat, who rushes to the head of the line, grabs his food and stomps over to a table by himself. “Who is that?” the new arrival asked St. Peter. “Oh, that’s God,” came the reply, “but sometimes he thinks he’s a doctor.”

③ If you are part of the group which you are addressing, you will be in a position to know the experiences and problems which are common to all of you and it’ll be appropriate for you to make a passing remark about the inedible canteen food or the chairman’s notorious bad taste in ties. With other audiences you mustn’t attempt to cut in with humor as they will resent an outsider making disparaging remarks about their canteen or their chairman. You will be on safer ground if you stick to scapegoats like the Post Office or the telephone system.

④ If you feel awkward being humorous, you must practice so that it becomes more natural. Include a few casual and apparently off-the-cuff remarks which you can deliver in a relaxed and unforced manner. Often it’s the delivery which causes the audience to smile, so speak slowly and remember that a raised eyebrow or an unbelieving look may help to show that you are making a light-hearted remark.

⑤ Look for the humor. It often comes from the unexpected. A twist on a familiar quote “If at first you don’t succeed, give up” or a play on words or on a situation. Search for exaggeration and understatement. Look at your talk and pick out a few words or sentences which you can turn about and inject with humor.

## 四、完整翻译
① 如果你想在演讲中运用幽默来逗人发笑，你必须知道如何识别听众的共同经历和共同问题。你的幽默必须与听众相关，并且应该有助于向他们表明你是他们中的一员，或者你理解他们的处境并赞同他们的观点。根据你演讲的对象不同，你们的共同问题也会有所不同。如果你在和一群经理讲话，你可以提及他们秘书杂乱无章的工作方式；反过来，如果你在对秘书们发表演说，你或许可以评论一下他们那些做事毫无条理的老板。
