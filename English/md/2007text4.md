## 一、中英文主旨
### 中文主旨
本文指出数据安全已从IT部门的边缘问题上升为企业高管的核心议程。通过列举多起重大数据泄露事件，说明保护客户数据是企业市场价值的关键，董事会需代表股东对此负责。文章还指出美国此前缺乏数据泄露法律处罚的现状正在改变，监管机构将对数据安全不力的企业采取行动。

### 英文主旨
This article points out that data security has risen from a marginal issue for IT departments to a core agenda for corporate executives. By citing multiple major data breach incidents, it illustrates that protecting customer data is the key to a company's market value, for which the board of directors is accountable on behalf of shareholders. It also notes that the previous lack of legal penalties for data leakage in the US is changing, and regulators will take action against firms that fail to provide adequate data security.

## 三、完整文章原文
It never rains but it pours. Just as bosses and boards have finally sorted out their worst accounting and compliance troubles, and improved their feeble corporation governance, a new problem threatens to earn them - especially in America - the sort of nasty headlines that inevitably lead to heads rolling in the executive suite: data insecurity. Left, until now, to odd, low-level IT staff to put right, and seen as a concern only of data-rich industries such as banking, telecoms and air travel, information protection is now high on the boss's agenda in businesses of every variety.

Several massive leakages of customer and employee data this year - from organizations as diverse as Time Warner, the American defense contractor Science Applications International Corp and even the University of California, Berkeley - have left managers hurriedly peering into their intricate IT systems and business processes in search of potential vulnerabilities.

"Data is becoming an asset which needs to be guarded as much as any other asset," says Haim Mendelson of Stanford University's business school. "The ability to guard customer data is the key to market value, which the board is responsible for on behalf of shareholders." Indeed, just as there is the concept of Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP), perhaps it is time for GASP, Generally Accepted Security Practices, suggested Eli Noam of New York's Columbia Business School. "Setting the proper investment level for security, redundancy, and recovery is a management issue, not a technical one," he says.

The mystery is that this should come as a surprise to any boss. Surely it should be obvious to the dimmest executive that trust, that most valuable of economic assets, is easily destroyed and hugely expensive to restore - and that few things are more likely to destroy trust than a company letting sensitive personal data get into the wrong hands.

The current state of affairs may have been encouraged - though not justified - by the lack of legal penalty (in America, but not Europe) for data leakage. Until California recently passed a law, American firms did not have to tell anyone, even the victim, when data went astray. That may change fast: lots of proposed data-security legislation is now doing the rounds in Washington, D.C. Meanwhile, the theft of information about some 40 million credit-card accounts in America, disclosed on June 17th, overshadowed a hugely important decision a day earlier by America's Federal Trade Commission (FTC) that puts corporate America on notice that regulators will act if firms fail to provide adequate data security.

## 四、完整文章翻译
不雨则已，一雨倾盆。就在老板和董事会终于解决了最棘手的会计和合规问题，并改善了薄弱的公司治理之际，一个新的问题正威胁着他们——尤其是在美国——让他们登上那种不可避免会导致高管层有人下台的负面头条：数据不安全。在此之前，信息保护一直交由临时的、低级别的IT员工来处理，并且被视为只有银行、电信和航空旅行这类数据密集型行业才需要关注的问题，而如今，它已成为各行各业老板议程上的头等大事。

今年发生了多起大规模的客户和员工数据泄露事件——涉及的机构五花八门，从时代华纳、美国国防承包商科学应用国际公司，甚至到加州大学伯克利分校——这使得管理者们匆忙检查他们复杂的IT系统和业务流程，以寻找潜在的安全漏洞。

斯坦福大学商学院的海姆·门德尔松说：“数据正在成为一种资产，需要像其他任何资产一样受到保护。保护客户数据的能力是市场价值的关键，董事会代表股东对此负责。”纽约哥伦比亚商学院的伊莱·诺姆认为，事实上，正如存在公认会计原则（GAAP）这一概念一样，或许现在是时候制定公认安全惯例（GASP）了。他说：“为安全、冗余和恢复设定适当的投资水平是一个管理问题，而不是技术问题。”

目前这种状况的形成，在一定程度上是由于（美国而非欧洲）对数据泄露缺乏法律处罚，尽管这并不能成为借口。在加州最近通过一项法律之前，美国公司在数据丢失时甚至不必告知任何人，包括受害者。这种情况可能很快就会改变：许多拟议的数据安全立法目前正在华盛顿特区进行审议。与此同时，6月17日披露的美国约4000万信用卡账户信息被盗事件，使得前一天美国联邦贸易委员会（FTC）做出的一项极其重要的决定黯然失色。该决定警告美国企业界，如果公司未能提供足够的数据安全保障，监管机构将采取行动。
