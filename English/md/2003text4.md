## 一、文章主旨
### 中文主旨
本文围绕美国医疗体系展开讨论，指出医疗技术的进步大幅提升了美国人的寿命与生活质量，但人们错误地将死亡视为可解决的问题，导致医疗支出失控、过度开展无效的激进治疗。文章反驳了“老年人有义务死亡为年轻人让路”的极端观点，同时提出社会医疗投入存在限度，美国应调整医疗资金分配，减少对渺茫治愈方案的过度投入，重视能切实改善生活的普通疗法研究。

### 英文主旨
This article discusses the US medical system. It points out that the advancement of medical technology has greatly improved Americans' life expectancy and quality of life, yet people mistakenly treat death as a solvable problem, leading to runaway medical spending and excessive ineffective aggressive treatments. The article refutes the extreme view that the elderly have a duty to die to make way for the young. Meanwhile, it argues that social medical investment has its limits. The US should adjust the allocation of medical funds, reduce overinvestment in unlikely cures, and attach importance to research on ordinary therapies that can practically improve people's lives.

## 三、全文原文
It is said that in England death is pressing, in Canada inevitable and in California optional. Small wonder. Americans’ life expectancy has nearly doubled over the past century. Failing hips can be replaced, clinical depression controlled, cataracts removed in a 30-minute surgical procedure. Such advances offer the aging population a quality of life that was unimaginable when I entered medicine 50 years ago. But not even a great health-care system can cure death — and our failure to confront that reality now threatens this greatness of ours.

Death is normal; we are genetically programmed to disintegrate and perish, even under ideal conditions. We all understand that at some level, yet as medical consumers we treat death as a problem to be solved. Shielded by third-party payers from the cost of our care, we demand everything that can possibly be done for us, even if it’s useless. The most obvious example is late-stage cancer care. Physicians — frustrated by their inability to cure the disease and fearing loss of hope in the patient — too often offer aggressive treatment far beyond what is scientifically justified.

In 1950, the US spent $12.7 billion on health care. In 2002, the cost will be $1,540 billion. Anyone can see this trend is unsustainable. Yet few seem willing to try to reverse it. Some scholars conclude that a government with finite resources should simply stop paying for medical care that sustains life beyond a certain age — say 83 or so. Former Colorado governor Richard Lamm has been quoted as saying that the old and infirm “have a duty to die and get out of the way”, so that younger, healthier people can realize their potential.

I would not go that far. Energetic people now routinely work through their 60s and beyond, and remain dazzlingly productive. At 78, Viacom chairman Sumner Redstone jokingly claims to be 53. Supreme Court Justice Sandra Day O’Connor is in her 70s, and former surgeon general C. Everett Koop chairs an Internet start-up in his 80s. These leaders are living proof that prevention works and that we can manage the health problems that come naturally with age. As a mere 68-year-old, I wish to age as productively as they have.

Yet there are limits to what a society can spend in this pursuit. As a physician, I know the most costly and dramatic measures may be ineffective and painful. I also know that people in Japan and Sweden, countries that spend far less on medical care, have achieved longer, healthier lives than we have. As a nation, we may be overfunding the quest for unlikely cures while underfunding research on humbler therapies that could improve people’s lives.

## 四、全文翻译
据说，在英国死亡是迫在眉睫的，在加拿大死亡是不可避免的，而在加利福尼亚死亡则是可以选择的。这不足为奇。在过去的一个世纪里，美国人的预期寿命几乎翻了一番。衰竭的髋关节可以置换，临床抑郁症可以得到控制，白内障只需30分钟的外科手术便可摘除。这些医学进步为老年人群带来了高品质的生活，这在我50年前进入医疗行业时是无法想象的。但即便是一流的医疗体系也无法治愈死亡——而我们如今无法正视这一现实，正威胁着我们医疗体系的这份卓越。

死亡是正常的；即便在理想状态下，我们的基因也注定了人体会逐渐衰亡。我们在某种程度上都明白这一点，然而作为医疗消费者，我们却将死亡视为一个有待解决的问题。由于有第三方支付方承担我们的医疗费用，我们要求用尽一切可能的医疗手段，即便这些手段毫无用处。最明显的例子就是晚期癌症治疗。医生们既因无法治愈这种疾病而沮丧，又担心病人失去希望，往往会采用远超科学合理范畴的激进治疗方案。

1950年，美国在医疗保健上的支出为127亿美元。到2002年，这一花费将达到15400亿美元。任何人都能看出，这种趋势是难以为继的。然而，似乎很少有人愿意扭转这一趋势。一些学者得出结论，资源有限的政府应当停止为超过一定年龄（比如83岁左右）的人群提供续命的医疗服务。有人援引科罗拉多州前州长理查德·拉姆的话称，年老体弱者“有义务死去，为年轻人让路”，以便更年轻、更健康的人能发挥自身潜力。

我不会如此极端。如今精力充沛的人通常工作到60多岁甚至更久，并且依然保持着极高的生产力。78岁的维亚康姆董事长萨姆纳·雷德斯通常开玩笑说自己只有53岁。最高法院大法官桑德拉·戴·奥康纳已70多岁，前卫生局局长C·埃弗雷特·库普80多岁时还执掌着一家互联网初创公司。这些领军人物就是鲜活的例证，证明预防是有效的，也证明我们能够应对随年龄增长自然出现的健康问题。作为一名年仅68岁的人，我希望自己老去时也能像他们一样保持高效。

然而，一个社会在这一追求上的投入是有限度的。作为一名医生，我知道最昂贵、最激进的医疗手段可能既无效又痛苦。我也知道，日本和瑞典这两个在医疗上投入少得多的国家，民众的寿命比我们更长，也更健康。作为一个国家，我们或许在寻求渺茫的治愈方法上投入过多，而对能改善民众生活的普通疗法研究投入不足。
