## 一、全文中英文主旨
### 中文主旨
本文以艺术领域的新潮流为切入点，核心探讨未来主义诗歌的文学属性问题。作者提出，当艺术新思潮成为潮流时，应先厘清其倡导者的核心主张，因为当下看似牵强无理的理论，未来或许会被视作常态；但针对未来主义诗歌，作者明确指出，即便承认其理论基础有合理之处，这类作品也很难被归入文学范畴。同时作者认可“情感生活的巨变需要表达方式的革新”这一前提，最终以反问的方式，从根本上否定了未来主义诗歌的文学价值。

### 英文主旨
This article takes the new trend in the art field as the entry point, and focuses on the literary attribute of Futurist poetry. The author proposes that when a new artistic trend becomes fashionable, we should first clarify the core proposition of its advocates, because the theory that seems far-fetched and unreasonable today may be regarded as normal in the future. However, for Futurist poetry, the author clearly points out that even if we admit that its theoretical basis is reasonable, such works can hardly be classified as literature. At the same time, the author acknowledges the premise that "great changes in emotional life call for innovation of expression", and finally fundamentally negates the literary value of Futurist poetry in a rhetorical way.

## 二、逐句精读解析（分段落）
### 第一段
#### 逐句翻译、核心词汇与长难句分析
1. **原句**：When a new movement in art attains a certain fashion, it is advisable to find out what its advocates are aiming at, for, however farfetched and unreasonable their principles may seem today, it is possible that in years to come they may be regarded as normal.
   - **译文**：当艺术领域的一场新运动形成某种潮流时，明智的做法是弄清其倡导者的核心意图，因为无论他们的准则在今天看来多么牵强、多么不合情理，未来数年这些准则都有可能被视作常态。
   - **常考单词&短语**：
     - movement n. （思想、艺术领域的）运动，思潮
     - attain v. 获得，达到；形成
     - fashion n. 时尚，潮流；风行一时的事物
     - advisable adj. 明智的，可取的
     - advocate n. 倡导者，拥护者 v. 提倡，拥护
     - aim at 旨在，以……为目标
     - farfetched adj. 牵强的，难以置信的
     - unreasonable adj. 不合理的，荒唐的
     - principle n. 原则，准则，信条
     - in years to come 未来数年，在将来
     - be regarded as 被视作，被认为
   - **长难句分析**：
     这是一个多重复合句，主句+时间状语从句+原因状语从句+让步状语从句。
     - 主句：`it is advisable to find out what its advocates are aiming at`（it作形式主语，真正的主语是后面的不定式`to find out...`；what引导宾语从句，作find out的宾语）
     - 时间状语从句：`When a new movement in art attains a certain fashion`（when引导，修饰主句动作发生的时间）
     - 原因状语从句：`for...it is possible that in years to come they may be regarded as normal`（for引导原因状语从句，解释主句建议的原因；从句中it作形式主语，真正主语是that引导的主语从句）
     - 让步状语从句：`however farfetched and unreasonable their principles may seem today`（however引导，相当于no matter how，修饰形容词farfetched和unreasonable，嵌入在原因状语从句中，表让步）

2. **原句**：With regard to Futurist poetry, however, the case is rather difficult, for whatever Futurist poetry may be — even admitting that the theory on which it is based may be right — it can hardly be classed as Literature.
   - **译文**：然而，就未来主义诗歌而言，情况却相当棘手，因为无论未来主义诗歌究竟是什么——即便我们承认它赖以成立的理论可能是正确的——它也很难被归入文学的范畴。
   - **常考单词&短语**：
     - with regard to 关于，就……而言
     - futurist adj. 未来主义的 n. 未来主义者
     - poetry n. 诗歌，诗作
     - case n. 情况，情形；案例
     - rather adv. 相当，非常
     - admit v. 承认；准许进入
     - be based on 以……为基础，基于
     - hardly adv. 几乎不，很难
     - be classed as 被归类为，被划分为
     - literature n. 文学，文学作品
   - **长难句分析**：
     这是一个并列复合句，主句+原因状语从句+双重让步结构+定语从句。
     - 主句：`the case is rather difficult`（however和With regard to...均为状语，修饰主句）
     - 原因状语从句：`for...it can hardly be classed as Literature`（for引导，解释主句的原因）
     - 让步结构1：`whatever Futurist poetry may be`（whatever引导，相当于no matter what，表让步）
     - 让步结构2：`even admitting that...may be right`（现在分词短语作让步状语，其中that引导宾语从句，作admitting的宾语）
     - 定语从句：`on which it is based`（介词+which引导，修饰先行词the theory，正常语序为it is based on the theory）

#### 本段核心概括
本段开篇提出通用观点——艺术新潮流兴起时应先厘清其核心主张，因为当下荒诞的理论未来可能被接受；随即转折提出全文核心论点：未来主义诗歌即便理论合理，也很难被归为文学。

### 第二段
#### 逐句翻译、核心词汇与长难句分析
1. **原句**：This, in brief, is what the Futurist says: for a century, past conditions of life have been conditionally speeding up, till now we live in a world of noise and violence and speed.
   - **译文**：简而言之，未来主义者这样宣称：一个世纪以来，过往的生活状况一直在有条件地加速发展，时至今日，我们生活在一个充斥着喧嚣、暴力与速度的世界之中。
   - **常考单词&短语**：
     - in brief 简而言之，总而言之
     - condition n. 状况，情况；条件
     - speed up 加速，加快
     - till conj. 直到……为止
     - violence n. 暴力，狂暴
   - **长难句分析**：
     复合句，主句+表语从句+冒号后的解释分句。
     - 主句：`This is what the Futurist says`（what引导表语从句，作is的表语；in brief是插入语）
     - 冒号后为未来主义者的观点，分为两个分句：前半句`for a century...speeding up`为现在完成进行时，表动作从过去持续到现在；后半句`till now...speed`为时间状语引导的主句，表最终结果。

2. **原句**：Consequently, our feelings, thoughts and emotions have undergone a corresponding change.
   - **译文**：因此，我们的感官、思想与情绪都随之发生了相应的变化。
   - **常考单词&短语**：
     - consequently adv. 因此，所以
     - undergo v. 经历，经受（变化、不快的事等）
     - corresponding adj. 相应的，符合的
     - emotion n. 情绪，情感

3. **原句**：This speeding up of life, says the Futurist, requires a new form of expression.
   - **译文**：未来主义者称，这种生活节奏的加快，需要一种全新的表达方式。
   - **常考单词&短语**：
     - speeding up 加速，提速
     - require v. 需要，要求
     - form n. 形式，方式
     - expression n. 表达，表达方式

4. **原句**：We must speed up our literature too, if we want to interpret modern stress.
   - **译文**：如果我们想要诠释现代社会的压力，就必须也让我们的文学加速发展。
   - **常考单词&短语**：
     - interpret v. 诠释，解读；口译
     - stress n. 压力，重压；强调
   - **长难句分析**：
     复合句，主句+if引导的条件状语从句，从句置于句末，表前提条件。

5. **原句**：We must pour out a large stream of essential words, unhampered by stops, or qualifying adjectives, or finite verbs.
   - **译文**：我们必须大量使用基本词汇，不受句号、修饰性形容词或限定动词的约束。
   - **常考单词&短语**：
     - pour out 大量涌出；倾吐，诉说
     - a stream of 一连串，大量
     - essential adj. 基本的，必不可少的
     - unhampered adj. 不受阻碍的，不受约束的
     - qualifying adj. 修饰性的，限定性的
     - adjective n. 形容词
     - finite verb 限定动词，谓语动词

6. **原句**：Instead of describing sounds we must make up words that imitate them; we must use many sizes of type and different colored inks on the same page, and shorten or lengthen words at will.
   - **译文**：我们不应描绘声音，而必须创造出模仿声音的词汇；我们必须在同一页面上使用多种字号、不同颜色的墨水，还可以随意缩短或拉长单词。
   - **常考单词&短语**：
     - instead of 而不是，代替
     - describe v. 描述，描绘
     - make up 编造，创造；组成；弥补
     - imitate v. 模仿，效仿
     - type n. 字体，字号；类型
     - ink n. 墨水，油墨
     - shorten v. 缩短，变短
     - lengthen v. 拉长，延长
     - at will 随意，任意
   - **长难句分析**：
     并列句，由分号连接两个并列分句。
     - 前一分句：`Instead of describing sounds we must make up words that imitate them`（that引导定语从句，修饰先行词words）
     - 后一分句：`we must use many sizes...and shorten or lengthen words at will`（包含两个并列的谓语must use和(can) shorten or lengthen，表未来主义者提出的并列要求）

#### 本段核心概括
本段完整阐述了未来主义者的核心主张：生活节奏的加速改变了人们的情感与思想，因此文学也必须加速革新，摒弃传统语法规则，创造全新的表达形式来适配现代生活。

#### 逐句翻译、核心词汇与长难句分析
1. **原句**：Certainly their descriptions of battles are confused.
   - **译文**：当然，他们对战争的描述是混乱难懂的。
   - **常考单词&短语**：

2. **原句**：But it is a little upsetting to read in the explanatory notes that a certain line describes a fight between a Turkish and a Bulgarian officer on a bridge off which they both fall into the river — and then to find that the line consists of the noise of their falling and the weights of the officers: “Pluff! Pluff! A hundred and eighty-five kilograms.”
   - **译文**：但当我们在注释中读到某一行诗描写了一名土耳其军官和一名保加利亚军官在桥上打斗，两人双双从桥上坠入河中，结果却发现这行诗只由他们落水的声响和军官的体重构成：“扑通！扑通！185公斤。”，这难免让人感到些许不适。
   - **常考单词&短语**：
     - upsetting adj. 令人不快的，令人心烦的
     - explanatory adj. 解释的，说明的
     - note n. 注释，注解；笔记
     - certain adj. 某一，某个；确定的
     - officer n. 军官；官员
     - fall into 落入，掉进
     - consist of 由……组成，由……构成
     - weight n. 重量，体重
   - **长难句分析**：
     这是全文核心长难句，主句为`it is a little upsetting to read...and then to find...`，it作形式主语，真正的主语是两个并列的不定式结构`to read...`和`to find...`。
     - 不定式1：`to read in the explanatory notes that...`（that引导宾语从句，作read的宾语）
       - 该宾语从句中，`off which they both fall into the river`是介词+which引导的定语从句，修饰先行词bridge，正常语序为`they both fall off the bridge into the river`
     - 不定式2：`to find that...`（that引导宾语从句，作find的宾语，和前面的to read由and then连接，构成并列主语）
     - 破折号的作用：分隔两个并列的不定式主语，突出前后内容的反差，强化作者的讽刺语气。

本段以具体的诗歌案例为例，直观展现了未来主义诗歌的混乱晦涩，通过诗句内容与注释的强烈反差，讽刺了这类作品的表达形式完全脱离了文学的本质。

## 三、完整原文
Text 3

When a new movement in art attains a certain fashion, it is advisable to find out what its advocates are aiming at, for, however farfetched and unreasonable their principles may seem today, it is possible that in years to come they may be regarded as normal. With regard to Futurist poetry, however, the case is rather difficult, for whatever Futurist poetry may be — even admitting that the theory on which it is based may be right — it can hardly be classed as Literature.

This, in brief, is what the Futurist says: for a century, past conditions of life have been conditionally speeding up, till now we live in a world of noise and violence and speed. Consequently, our feelings, thoughts and emotions have undergone a corresponding change. This speeding up of life, says the Futurist, requires a new form of expression. We must speed up our literature too, if we want to interpret modern stress. We must pour out a large stream of essential words, unhampered by stops, or qualifying adjectives, or finite verbs. Instead of describing sounds we must make up words that imitate them; we must use many sizes of type and different colored inks on the same page, and shorten or lengthen words at will.

Certainly their descriptions of battles are confused. But it is a little upsetting to read in the explanatory notes that a certain line describes a fight between a Turkish and a Bulgarian officer on a bridge off which they both fall into the river — and then to find that the line consists of the noise of their falling and the weights of the officers: “Pluff! Pluff! A hundred and eighty-five kilograms.”

This, though it fulfills the laws and requirements of Futurist poetry, can hardly be classed as Literature. All the same, no thinking man can refuse to accept their first proposition: that a great change in our emotional life calls for a change of expression. The whole question is really this: have we essentially changed?

当艺术领域的一场新运动形成某种潮流时，明智的做法是弄清其倡导者的核心意图，因为无论他们的准则在今天看来多么牵强、多么不合情理，未来数年这些准则都有可能被视作常态。然而，就未来主义诗歌而言，情况却相当棘手，因为无论未来主义诗歌究竟是什么——即便我们承认它赖以成立的理论可能是正确的——它也很难被归入文学的范畴。
