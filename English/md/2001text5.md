# 2001text5

## 一、文章主旨
### 中文主旨
本文讲述了作者辞去全职工作后，从狂热追求“拥有一切”的生活哲学转变为拥抱“放慢生活节奏”的亲身经历，并对比分析了英美两国出现“放慢生活节奏”这一社会趋势的不同根源，最终指出这种简单的生活方式所带来的精神回报，远远超过了经济成功和社会地位。

### 英文主旨

This article describes the author's personal experience of transforming from a passionate advocate of the philosophy of "having it all" to embracing "downshifting" after quitting her full-time job. It compares and analyzes the different roots of the social trend of downshifting in Britain and America, and finally points out that the spiritual rewards brought by this simple lifestyle far outweigh financial success and social status.

## 三、完整原文
Text 5

When I decided to quit my full time employment it never occurred to me that I might become a part of a new international trend. A lateral move that hurt my pride and blocked my professional progress prompted me to abandon my relatively high profile career although, in the manner of a disgraced government minister, I covered my exit by claiming "I wanted to spend more time with my family".

Curiously, some two-and-a-half years and two novels later, my experiment in what the Americans term "downshifting" has turned my tired excuse into an absolute reality. I have been transformed from a passionate advocate of the philosophy of "having it all", preached by Linda Kelsey for the past seven years in the pages of She magazine, into a woman who is happy to settle for a bit of everything.

I have discovered, as perhaps Kelsey will after her much-publicized resignation from the editorship of She after a build-up of stress, that abandoning the doctrine of "juggling your life", and making the alternative move into "downshifting" brings with it far greater rewards than financial success and social status. Nothing could persuade me to return to the kind of life Kelsey used to advocate and I once enjoyed: 12-hour working days, pressured deadlines, the fearful strain of office politics and the limitations of being a parent on "quality time".

In America, the move away from juggling to a simpler, less materialistic lifestyle is a well-established trend. Downshifting-also known in America as "voluntary simplicity"-has, ironically, even bred a new area of what might be termed anticonsumerism. There are a number of bestselling downshifting self-help books for people who want to simplify their lives; there are newsletters, such as The Tightwad Gazette, that give hundreds of thousands of Americans useful tips on anything from recycling their cling-film to making their own soap; there are even support groups for those who want to achieve the mid-'90s equivalent of dropping out.

While in America the trend started as a reaction to the economic decline after the mass redundancies caused by downsizing in the late '80s—and is still linked to the politics of thrift, in Britain, at least among the middle-class downshifters of my acquaintance, we have different reasons for seeking to simplify our lives.

For the women of my generation who were urged to keep juggling through the '80s, downshifting in the mid-'90s is not so much a search for the mythical good life—growing your own organic vegetables, and risking turning into one—as a personal recognition of your limitations.

### Text 5 译文
当我决定辞去全职工作时，我从未想到自己会成为一种新的国际潮流的一部分。一次伤了我自尊、阻碍了我职业发展的平级调动促使我放弃了那份相对引人注目的职业。不过，我以一位失势政府部长的方式，声称“我想多陪陪家人”来掩饰自己的离职。

奇怪的是，大约两年半后，在我写完两部小说之后，我这种被美国人称为“放慢生活节奏”的尝试，已经把我那老掉牙的借口变成了绝对的现实。我已经从一个狂热倡导“拥有一切”生活哲学的人，变成了一个乐于对一切都浅尝辄止的女人。而“拥有一切”的哲学是琳达·凯尔西过去七年在《她》杂志上一直宣扬的。

我发现，放弃“为生活奔波”的信条，转而选择“放慢生活节奏”，所带来的回报远比经济成功和社会地位要丰厚得多。或许凯尔西在因压力过大而高调辞去《她》杂志主编一职后也会有同样的发现。没有什么能说服我回到凯尔西过去倡导、我也曾享受过的那种生活：每天12小时的工作时间、令人倍感压力的最后期限、办公室政治带来的可怕压力，以及作为父母只能在“黄金时间”陪伴孩子的局限。

在美国，从为生活奔波转向更简单、更少物质主义的生活方式已经成为一种根深蒂固的趋势。具有讽刺意味的是，放慢生活节奏——在美国也被称为“自愿简朴”——甚至催生了一个可以被称为反消费主义的新领域。有许多关于放慢生活节奏的畅销自助书籍，供那些想要简化生活的人阅读；还有像《吝啬鬼公报》这样的通讯刊物，给数十万美国人提供各种实用建议，从回收保鲜膜到自制肥皂；甚至还有一些互助小组，帮助那些想要实现90年代中期那种“遁世”生活的人。

在美国，这一趋势最初是对80年代末企业裁员导致大规模失业后的经济衰退的一种反应，并且至今仍与节俭政治相关；而在英国，至少在我认识的中产阶级放慢生活节奏者当中，我们寻求简化生活的原因却有所不同。

对于我们这一代在80年代被敦促要不断为生活奔波的女性来说，90年代中期的放慢生活节奏，与其说是在寻找那种神话般的美好生活——自己种有机蔬菜，甚至冒着变成“有机蔬菜”的风险——不如说是对自身局限性的一种个人认知。
