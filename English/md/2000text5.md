## 一、文章中英文主旨
### 中文主旨
本文围绕“抱负（ambition）”这一核心话题展开，批判了受过教育的群体对抱负心口不一的虚伪态度，剖析了当下人们不敢公开承认自身抱负的社会现象，指出对抱负的多方攻击使其从公开推崇转为私下奉行，同时揭示了这一现象背后的社会心态与虚伪本质。
### 英文主旨
This article centers on the core topic of ambition. It criticizes the hypocritical duplicity of the educated group towards ambition, analyzes the social phenomenon that people dare not openly admit their ambition nowadays, points out that multi-angle attacks on ambition have made it shift from open praise to private pursuit, and reveals the social mentality and hypocritical nature behind this phenomenon.

## 二、逐句精读解析（分段落）
### 第一段
1.  **原文**：If ambition is to be well regarded, the rewards of ambition — wealth, distinction, control over one’s destiny — must be deemed worthy of the sacrifices made on ambition’s behalf.
    **译文**：如果要让抱负受人敬重，那么它所带来的回报——财富、声望、对个人命运的掌控——就必须被认为值得人们为了抱负所做出的种种牺牲。
    **常考单词&短语**：
    - ambition n. 抱负，野心，雄心（考研核心词）
    - well regarded 受到好评的，受人敬重的
    - reward n. 回报，奖赏 v. 奖励，回报
    - distinction n. 声望，殊荣；区别，差别（考研多义核心词，文中取“声望”义）
    - destiny n. 命运，天命
    - deem v. 认为，视为（正式用语，考研高频词）
    - worthy of 值得……的
    - sacrifice n. 牺牲，献祭 v. 牺牲，献出
    - on behalf of sb / on one’s behalf 为了某人的利益，代表某人
    **长难句结构分析**：
    本句为带条件状语从句的复合句，核心结构如下：
    - 条件状语从句：If ambition is to be well regarded（If引导，表前提条件）
    - 主句主干：the rewards of ambition must be deemed worthy of the sacrifices
    - 插入同位语：破折号之间的wealth, distinction, control over one’s destiny，补充说明the rewards of ambition的具体内容
    - 后置定语：made on ambition’s behalf为过去分词短语，修饰the sacrifices，明确牺牲的目的

2.  **原文**：If the tradition of ambition is to have vitality, it must be widely shared; and it especially must be highly regarded by people who are themselves admired, the educated not least among them.
    **译文**：如果抱负的传统想要具有生命力，就必须被人们广泛接纳；它尤其必须受到那些本身就受人敬仰的人们的高度重视，其中受过良好教育的人更是不在少数。
    **常考单词&短语**：
    - vitality n. 活力，生命力
    - highly regarded 高度重视，深受好评
    - admire v. 钦佩，敬仰，赞美
    - the educated 受过教育的人（the+形容词表一类人，考研高频语法点）
    - not least 尤其，相当重要地
    **长难句结构分析**：
    本句为分号+and连接的并列复合句，结构如下：
    - 前半分句：If引导条件状语从句，主句为it must be widely shared
    - 后半分句：主句为it especially must be highly regarded by people；who引导限制性定语从句who are themselves admired，修饰先行词people；the educated not least among them为独立主格结构，作补充状语，进一步限定people的范围

3.  **原文**：In an odd way, however, it is the educated who have claimed to have given up on ambition as an ideal.
    **译文**：然而，奇怪的是，恰恰是这些受过教育的人，声称已经放弃了将抱负作为一种理想。
    **常考单词&短语**：
    - odd adj. 奇怪的，怪异的；奇数的；偶然的
    - claim v. 声称，宣称；索要 n. 声明，主张；索赔
    - give up on 放弃，对……不抱希望
    - ideal n. 理想，典范 adj. 理想的，完美的
    **长难句结构分析**：
    本句为**强调句**，核心结构为it is + 被强调部分（the educated） + who + 剩余内容。去掉it is...who后，句子the educated have claimed to have given up on ambition as an ideal结构完整，符合强调句的判定规则。

4.  **原文**：What is odd is that they have perhaps most benefited from ambition — if not always their own then that of their parents and grandparents.
    **译文**：更离奇的是，他们或许大多正是从抱负中获益的——即便不总是源于他们自己的抱负，那也是源于他们父母和祖父母的抱负。
    **常考单词&短语**：
    - benefit from 从……中获益，得益于（考研高频短语）
    - grandparents n. 祖父母，外祖父母
    **长难句结构分析**：
    本句为主系表结构，核心如下：
    - 主语：What引导的主语从句What is odd
    - 系动词：is
    - 表语：that引导的表语从句that they have perhaps most benefited from ambition
    - 插入补充：破折号后的内容为补充说明，其中that为代词，指代前文的ambition，避免重复，完整还原为if not always their own ambition then the ambition of their parents and grandparents

5.  **原文**：There is a heavy note of hypocrisy in this, a case of closing the barn door after the horses have escaped — with the educated themselves riding on them.
    **译文**：这其中带有浓重的虚伪色彩，就好比马跑了之后才关上马厩的门——而受过教育的人自己正骑在那些马上。
    **常考单词&短语**：
    - hypocrisy n. 虚伪，伪善（对应形容词hypocritical，下文高频出现）
    - note n. 语气，色彩；笔记；音符；注释
    - barn n. 谷仓，畜棚，马厩
    - escape v. 逃跑，逃离；逃避 n. 逃跑，逃避
    **长难句结构分析**：
    本句主干为There is a heavy note of hypocrisy in this；a case of closing the barn door after the horses have escaped为同位语，用俗语比喻前文的虚伪现象，其中after引导时间状语从句；破折号后的with the educated themselves riding on them为with独立主格结构，作伴随状语，them指代前文的the horses，强化讽刺意味。

#### 本段核心概括
本段开篇提出抱负受人敬重的核心前提，指出受过教育的群体本应是推崇抱负的核心人群，却声称已放弃抱负，而他们恰恰是抱负的直接受益者，尖锐批判了这种“获益后反踩一脚”的虚伪行为。

### 第二段
1.  **原文**：Certainly people do not seem less interested in success and its signs now than formerly.
    **译文**：当然，如今人们对成功及其标志的兴趣，似乎并不比从前有所减少。
    **常考单词&短语**：
    - formerly adv. 从前，以前，以往
    - sign n. 标志，迹象；符号；手势 v. 签名，签署

2.  **原文**：Summer homes, European travel, BMWs — the locations, place names and name brands may change, but such items do not seem less in demand today than a decade or two years ago.
    **译文**：避暑别墅、欧洲旅行、宝马汽车——这些场所、地名和品牌或许会有变化，但如今人们对这类东西的需求，似乎并不比一二十年前有所减少。
    **常考单词&短语**：
    - summer home 避暑别墅，度假屋
    - brand n. 品牌，商标 v. 加污名于；打烙印于
    - in demand 受欢迎的，有需求的
    - decade n. 十年，十年期（考研核心词）
    **长难句结构分析**：
    本句为but连接的并列复合句。破折号前为成功标志的具体举例；破折号后前半分句为the locations, place names and name brands may change；后半分句用比较级的否定形式表肯定含义，do not seem less in demand than... 等同于“需求和从前一样旺盛”，是考研阅读高频的语义考点。

3.  **原文**：What has happened is that people cannot confess fully to their dreams, as easily and openly as once they could, lest they be thought pushing, acquisitive and vulgar.
    **译文**：而实际情况是，人们无法像从前那样轻松、公开地全然坦承自己的梦想，唯恐被人认为自己急功近利、贪得无厌、庸俗不堪。
    **常考单词&短语**：
    - confess v. 承认，坦白；忏悔（固定搭配confess to sth/doing sth 承认某事/做某事）
    - lest conj. 唯恐，以免（考研高频语法词，后接从句常用虚拟语气）
    - pushing adj. 急功近利的（文中贬义）；有进取心的
    - acquisitive adj. 贪得无厌的，渴求获取财物的
    - vulgar adj. 庸俗的，粗俗的；通俗的
    **长难句结构分析**：
    本句为主系表结构，核心如下：
    - 主语：What引导的主语从句What has happened
    - 系动词：is
    - 表语：that引导的表语从句that people cannot confess fully to their dreams
    - 比较状语：as easily and openly as once they could，修饰confess，说明坦承梦想的程度
    - 目的状语从句：lest引导，从句使用虚拟语气，完整形式为lest they (should) be thought pushing, acquisitive and vulgar，省略了情态动词should，是考研核心语法考点

4.  **原文**：Instead, we are treated to fine hypocritical spectacles, which now more than ever seem in ample supply: the critic of American materialism with a Southampton summer home; the publisher of radical books who takes his meals in three-star restaurants; the journalist advocating participatory democracy in all phases of life, whose own children are enrolled in private schools.
    **译文**：相反，我们看到了种种虚伪至极的表演，如今这类现象比以往任何时候都要多：抨击美国物质主义的批评家，却拥有南安普顿的避暑别墅；出版激进书籍的出版商，却在三星级餐厅用餐；鼓吹在人生各个阶段都应实行参与式民主的记者，自己的孩子却就读于私立学校。
    **常考单词&短语**：
    - be treated to 被款待；看到，目睹（文中引申义）
    - spectacle n. 光景，场面；奇观；表演（贬义时指“可笑的表演”）
    - ample adj. 大量的，充裕的，丰富的
    - in ample supply 供应充足，大量存在
    - critic n. 批评家，评论家；批评者
    - materialism n. 物质主义，唯物主义
    - radical adj. 激进的，根本的，彻底的 n. 激进分子
    - advocate v. 提倡，拥护，鼓吹 n. 倡导者，拥护者
    - participatory democracy 参与式民主
    - phase n. 阶段，时期，方面
    - enroll v. （使）入学，注册，登记（固定搭配enroll in 入学，加入）
    - private school 私立学校
    **长难句结构分析**：
    本句主干为we are treated to fine hypocritical spectacles；which引导非限制性定语从句which now more than ever seem in ample supply，修饰先行词spectacles；冒号后为三个并列的名词短语，作spectacles的同位语，用排比举例具象化“虚伪的表演”，三个并列结构分别为：
    1.  the critic of American materialism with a Southampton summer home
    2.  the publisher of radical books who takes his meals in three-star restaurants（who引导定语从句修饰publisher）
    3.  the journalist advocating participatory democracy in all phases of life, whose own children are enrolled in private schools（advocating现在分词作后置定语修饰journalist，whose引导定语从句修饰journalist）

5.  **原文**：For such people and many more perhaps not so exceptional, the proper formulation is, “Succeed at all costs but avoid appearing ambitious.”
    **译文**：对于这些人，以及更多或许没那么特立独行的人而言，最恰当的处世准则就是：“不惜一切代价获得成功，但要避免让人看出自己有抱负。”
    **常考单词&短语**：
    - exceptional adj. 例外的，特立独行的；杰出的
    - formulation n. 表述，准则；构想，配方
    - at all costs 不惜一切代价，无论如何（考研高频短语）
    - appear v. 显得，似乎；出现，呈现

#### 本段核心概括
本段指出人们对成功的需求从未衰减，却因害怕被贴上负面标签，不敢公开坦承自己的抱负，并用三组极具讽刺性的现实案例，揭露了社会上普遍存在的“一边疯狂追求成功，一边伪装淡泊抱负”的双标与虚伪。

### 第三段
1.  **原文**：The attacks on ambition are many and come from various angles; its public defenders are few and unimpressive, where they are not extremely unattractive.
    **译文**：对抱负的攻击不胜枚举，且来自各个角度；公开为抱负辩护的人却寥寥无几，就算不是毫无吸引力，也难以给人留下深刻印象。
    **常考单词&短语**：
    - attack n. & v. 攻击，抨击；袭击
    - various adj. 各种各样的，多方面的
    - angle n. 角度，立场，视角
    - defender n. 辩护者，拥护者；防守者
    - unimpressive adj. 给人印象不深的，平淡无奇的
    - extremely adv. 极其，非常，极端地
    - unattractive adj. 无吸引力的，不招人喜欢的
    **长难句结构分析**：
    本句为分号连接的并列句，前后形成强烈对比。前半分句主干为The attacks on ambition are many and come from various angles；后半分句主干为its public defenders are few and unimpressive，where引导状语从句，where此处等同于in which case，表“即便在……的情况下”，补充说明辩护者的弱势处境。

2.  **原文**：As a result, the support for ambition as a healthy impulse, a quality to be admired and fixed in the mind of the young, is probably lower than it has ever been in the United States.
    **译文**：因此，在美国，将抱负视为一种健康的动力、一种值得被年轻人敬佩并深植于心的品质，这种支持度或许降到了前所未有的低点。
    **常考单词&短语**：
    - as a result 因此，结果（考研高频逻辑短语）
    - impulse n. 动力，冲动，推动力
    - quality n. 品质，特质；质量
    - fix v. 使固定，牢记；安装；修理
    **长难句结构分析**：
    本句主干为the support for ambition is probably lower than it has ever been in the United States。as a healthy impulse, a quality to be admired and fixed in the mind of the young为介词短语作后置定语，修饰ambition；其中a quality是a healthy impulse的同位语，to be admired and fixed in the mind of the young为不定式被动结构作后置定语，修饰a quality；than引导比较状语从句。

3.  **原文**：This does not mean that ambition is at an end, that people no longer feel its stirrings and promptings, but only that, no longer openly honored, it is less openly professed.
    **译文**：这并不意味着抱负已经穷途末路，也不意味着人们不再感受到它的鼓动与激励，而只是意味着，它不再被公开推崇，也就更少被公开宣扬。
    **常考单词&短语**：
    - at an end 完结，穷途末路，结束
    - stirring n. 鼓动，萌动，涌动 adj. 激动人心的
    - prompting n. 激励，催促，提示
    - honor v. 尊重，推崇，给……以荣誉 n. 荣誉，光荣
    - profess v. 宣称，公开表明，声称
    **长难句结构分析**：
    本句为并列宾语从句结构，主干为This does not mean that..., that..., but only that...，三个that引导的宾语从句并列作mean的宾语，前两个为否定含义，第三个用but only表转折，是核心语义落点。其中no longer openly honored为过去分词短语作原因状语，修饰it is less openly professed，it指代ambition。

4.  **原文**：Consequences follow from this, of course, some of which are that ambition is driven underground, or made sly.
    **译文**：当然，这随之带来了一些后果，其中一些便是，抱负被迫转入地下，或是变得偷偷摸摸。
    **常考单词&短语**：
    - consequence n. 结果，后果，影响（考研核心词）
    - follow from 由……产生，由……推断
    - underground adj. & adv. 地下的；秘密的，暗地里
    - sly adj. 偷偷摸摸的，狡猾的，诡秘的
    **长难句结构分析**：
    本句主干为Consequences follow from this。some of which引导非限制性定语从句，修饰先行词Consequences；定语从句中，that引导表语从句that ambition is driven underground, or made sly，其中made sly是is made sly的省略，和driven underground并列作从句的谓语。

5.  **原文**：Such, then, is the way things stand: on the left angry critics, on the right stupid supporters, and in the middle, as usual, the majority of earnest people trying to get on in life.
    **译文**：于是，情况就是这样：左边是愤怒的批评者，右边是愚蠢的支持者，而中间，和往常一样，是大多数认真努力、想要在生活中出人头地的人。
    **常考单词&短语**：
    - stand v. 处于（某种状态/情形）；站立；忍受
    - the majority of 大多数的，大部分的
    - earnest adj. 认真的，诚挚的，热切的
    - get on in life 出人头地，在生活中取得成功，发迹
    **长难句结构分析**：
    本句为倒装句，主干为Such is the way，then为插入语，things stand是省略了关系代词that的定语从句，修饰the way。冒号后为三个并列的介词短语构成的倒装结构，补充说明the way的具体内容，正常语序为angry critics are on the left, stupid supporters are on the right, and the majority of earnest people...are in the middle，将地点状语前置并省略be动词，实现结构平衡与节奏强化。

#### 本段核心概括
本段指出对抱负的公开抨击多、辩护少，导致社会对抱负的正面认可度降至低点，抱负不再被公开推崇和宣扬，被迫转入地下；最终描绘了当下社会中对抱负持不同态度的三类人群，收束全文。

## 三、完整原文
Text 5
1) If ambition is to be well regarded, the rewards of ambition — wealth, distinction, control over one’s destiny — must be deemed worthy of the sacrifices made on ambition’s behalf. If the tradition of ambition is to have vitality, it must be widely shared; and it especially must be highly regarded by people who are themselves admired, the educated not least among them. In an odd way, however, it is the educated who have claimed to have given up on ambition as an ideal. What is odd is that they have perhaps most benefited from ambition — if not always their own then that of their parents and grandparents. There is a heavy note of hypocrisy in this, a case of closing the barn door after the horses have escaped — with the educated themselves riding on them.

2) Certainly people do not seem less interested in success and its signs now than formerly. Summer homes, European travel, BMWs — the locations, place names and name brands may change, but such items do not seem less in demand today than a decade or two years ago. What has happened is that people cannot confess fully to their dreams, as easily and openly as once they could, lest they be thought pushing, acquisitive and vulgar. Instead, we are treated to fine hypocritical spectacles, which now more than ever seem in ample supply: the critic of American materialism with a Southampton summer home; the publisher of radical books who takes his meals in three-star restaurants; the journalist advocating participatory democracy in all phases of life, whose own children are enrolled in private schools. For such people and many more perhaps not so exceptional, the proper formulation is, “Succeed at all costs but avoid appearing ambitious.”

3) The attacks on ambition are many and come from various angles; its public defenders are few and unimpressive, where they are not extremely unattractive. As a result, the support for ambition as a healthy impulse, a quality to be admired and fixed in the mind of the young, is probably lower than it has ever been in the United States. This does not mean that ambition is at an end, that people no longer feel its stirrings and promptings, but only that, no longer openly honored, it is less openly professed. Consequences follow from this, of course, some of which are that ambition is driven underground, or made sly. Such, then, is the way things stand: on the left angry critics, on the right stupid supporters, and in the middle, as usual, the majority of earnest people trying to get on in life.

## 四、完整译文
### Text 5 全文翻译
1) 如果要让抱负受人敬重，那么它所带来的回报——财富、声望、对个人命运的掌控——就必须被认为值得人们为了抱负所做出的种种牺牲。如果抱负的传统想要具有生命力，就必须被人们广泛接纳；它尤其必须受到那些本身就受人敬仰的人们的高度重视，其中受过良好教育的人更是不在少数。然而，奇怪的是，恰恰是这些受过教育的人，声称已经放弃了将抱负作为一种理想。更离奇的是，他们或许大多正是从抱负中获益的——即便不总是源于他们自己的抱负，那也是源于他们父母和祖父母的抱负。这其中带有浓重的虚伪色彩，就好比马跑了之后才关上马厩的门——而受过教育的人自己正骑在那些马上。

2) 当然，如今人们对成功及其标志的兴趣，似乎并不比从前有所减少。避暑别墅、欧洲旅行、宝马汽车——这些场所、地名和品牌或许会有变化，但如今人们对这类东西的需求，似乎并不比一二十年前有所减少。而实际情况是，人们无法像从前那样轻松、公开地全然坦承自己的梦想，唯恐被人认为自己急功近利、贪得无厌、庸俗不堪。相反，我们看到了种种虚伪至极的表演，如今这类现象比以往任何时候都要多：抨击美国物质主义的批评家，却拥有南安普顿的避暑别墅；出版激进书籍的出版商，却在三星级餐厅用餐；鼓吹在人生各个阶段都应实行参与式民主的记者，自己的孩子却就读于私立学校。对于这些人，以及更多或许没那么特立独行的人而言，最恰当的处世准则就是：“不惜一切代价获得成功，但要避免让人看出自己有抱负。”

3) 对抱负的攻击不胜枚举，且来自各个角度；公开为抱负辩护的人却寥寥无几，就算不是毫无吸引力，也难以给人留下深刻印象。因此，在美国，将抱负视为一种健康的动力、一种值得被年轻人敬佩并深植于心的品质，这种支持度或许降到了前所未有的低点。这并不意味着抱负已经穷途末路，也不意味着人们不再感受到它的鼓动与激励，而只是意味着，它不再被公开推崇，也就更少被公开宣扬。当然，这随之带来了一些后果，其中一些便是，抱负被迫转入地下，或是变得偷偷摸摸。于是，情况就是这样：左边是愤怒的批评者，右边是愚蠢的支持者，而中间，和往常一样，是大多数认真努力、想要在生活中出人头地的人。
