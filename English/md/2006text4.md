## 反快乐艺术的兴起 2006text4

## 一、中英文主旨
### 中文主旨
本文探讨了艺术家为何愈发倾向于聚焦负面情绪进行创作，指出艺术并非因现代社会苦难更多而变得怀疑快乐，恰恰是因为当下商业文化与大众传媒充斥着泛滥的、功利化的虚假快乐，艺术需要承担起类似昔日宗教的功能，提醒人们正视人生的失落与终局，理解真正的快乐并非回避痛苦，而是与缺憾共存。

### 英文主旨

This article explores why artists increasingly tend to create works focusing on negative emotions. It points out that art has become skeptical of happiness not because modern society has more suffering, but precisely because the current commercial culture and mass media are filled with overflowing, utilitarian and false happiness. Art needs to assume the function similar to that of religion in the past, reminding people to face up to the loss and end of life, and understand that true happiness does not mean avoiding pain, but living with imperfection.

## 三、完整原文
Many things make people think artists are weird. But the weirdest may be this: artists’ only job is to explore emotions, and yet they choose to focus on the ones that feel bad.

This wasn’t always so. The earliest forms of art, like painting and music, are those best suited for expressing joy. But somewhere from the 19th century onward, more artists began seeing happiness as meaningless, phony or, worst of all, boring, as we went from Wordsworth’s daffodils to Baudelaire’s flowers of evil.

You could argue that art became more skeptical of happiness because modern times have seen so much misery. But it’s not as if earlier times didn’t know perpetual war, disaster and the massacre of innocents. The reason, in fact, may be just the opposite: there is too much damn happiness in the world today.

After all, what is the one modern form of expression almost completely dedicated to depicting happiness? Advertising. The rise of anti-happy art almost exactly tracks the emergence of mass media, and with it, a commercial culture in which happiness is not just an ideal but an ideology.

People in earlier eras were surrounded by reminders of misery. They worked until exhausted, lived with few protections and died young. In the West, before mass communication and literacy, the most powerful mass medium was the church, which reminded worshippers that their souls were in danger and that they would someday be meat for worms. Given all this, they did not exactly need their art to be a bummer too.

Today the messages the average Westerner is surrounded with are not religious but commercial, and forever happy. Fast-food eaters, news anchors, text messengers, all smiling, smiling, smiling. Our magazines feature beaming celebrities and happy families in perfect homes. And since these messages have an agenda — to lure us to open our wallets — they make the very idea of happiness seem unreliable. “Celebrate!” commanded the ads for the arthritis drug Celebrex, before we found out it could increase the risk of heart attacks.

But what we forget — what our economy depends on us forgetting — is that happiness is more than pleasure without pain. The things that bring the greatest joy carry the greatest potential for loss and disappointment. Today, surrounded by promises of easy happiness, we need art to tell us, as religion once did, Memento mori: remember that you will die, that everything ends, and that happiness comes not in denying this but in living with it. It’s a message even more bitter than a clove cigarette, yet, somehow, a breath of fresh air.

## 四、完整译文
许多事情让人们觉得艺术家很古怪。但最古怪的或许是这一点：艺术家唯一的工作就是探究各种情感，然而他们却选择聚焦于那些令人不快的情感。

情况并非一直如此。最早的艺术形式，比如绘画和音乐，是最适合表达喜悦的。但从19世纪的某个时期开始，越来越多的艺术家开始把快乐视为毫无意义、虚假虚伪，甚至最糟糕的是，枯燥乏味的东西，正如我们从华兹华斯的《水仙花》转向波德莱尔的《恶之花》所展现的那样。

你可能会辩称，艺术之所以对快乐愈发持怀疑态度，是因为现代社会经历了太多的苦难。但这并非是说，早些年代就没有无休止的战争、灾难和对无辜者的屠杀。事实上，原因或许恰恰相反：当今世界充斥着太多泛滥的快乐。

归根结底，几乎完全致力于描绘快乐的唯一现代表达形式是什么？是广告。反快乐艺术的兴起，几乎与大众传媒的出现完全同步，与之相伴而生的，还有一种商业文化——在这种文化中，快乐不仅是一种理想，更是一种意识形态。

早期时代的人们，身边处处都是苦难的提醒。他们工作到筋疲力尽，生活几乎没有保障，年纪轻轻便撒手人寰。在西方，在大众传播和教育普及之前，最具影响力的大众媒介是教堂，它提醒信徒们，他们的灵魂处于危险之中，他们终有一天会沦为蛆虫的食物。在这种情况下，他们完全不需要艺术再去增添一份令人沮丧的体验了。

如今，普通西方人身边充斥的信息不再与宗教相关，而是商业相关的，并且永远都在宣扬快乐。吃快餐的人、新闻主播、发短信的人，所有人都在笑，笑个不停。我们的杂志上，满是容光焕发的名人和住在完美住宅里的幸福家庭。而由于这些信息都有着一个目的——引诱我们打开钱包——它们使得“快乐”这个概念本身都显得不可靠。关节炎药物西乐葆的广告高呼着“庆祝吧！”，而后我们却发现，这款药物会增加心脏病发作的风险。

但我们所忘记的——我们的经济指望我们忘却的——是：快乐远不止是没有痛苦的欢愉。那些带来极致快乐的事物，也同时蕴藏着带来失落与失望的最大可能。如今，我们被“轻松获得快乐”的承诺包围着，我们需要艺术像曾经的宗教那样，告诉我们“人终有一死”：记住你终将逝去，万事皆有终局，快乐不在于否认这一点，而在于与之共存。这一启示甚至比丁香烟还要苦涩，然而不知为何，却带来了一股清新的气息。
