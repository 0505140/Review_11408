## 一、中英文主旨
### 中文主旨
本文探讨了习惯与创新的辩证关系，指出旧习惯虽难以根除，但有意识地培养新习惯能在大脑中生成新的神经通路，从而激发创造力；同时批判了标准化考试对创新与协作思维的压制，强调发挥自身优势并培养新习惯才能成就卓越。

### 英文主旨
This article explores the dialectical relationship between habits and innovation, pointing out that although old habits are hard to eradicate, consciously developing new habits can generate new neural pathways in the brain and thus stimulate creativity. It also criticizes the suppression of innovative and collaborative thinking by standardized testing, and emphasizes that exerting one's strengths and cultivating new habits leads to excellence.

## 三、完整文章原文
Habits are a funny thing. We reach for them mindlessly, setting our brains on auto-pilot and relaxing into the unconscious comfort of familiar routine. "Not choice, but habit rules the unreflecting herd," William Wordsworth said in the 19th century. In the ever-changing 21st century, even the word "habit" carries a negative implication.

So it seems paradoxical to talk about habits in the same context as creativity and innovation. But brain researchers have discovered that when we consciously develop new habits, we create parallel paths, and even entirely new brain cells, that can jump our trains of thought onto new, innovative tracks.

Rather than dismissing ourselves as unchangeable creatures of habit, we can instead direct our own change by consciously developing new habits. In fact, the more new things we try – the more we step outside our comfort zone – the more inherently creative we become, both in the workplace and in our personal lives.

But don't bother trying to kill off old habits; once those ruts of procedure are worn into the brain, they're there to stay. Instead, the new habits we deliberately press into ourselves create parallel pathways that can bypass those old roads.

"The first thing needed for innovation is a fascination with wonder," says Dawna Markova, author of The Open Mind. "But we are taught instead to 'decide', just as our president calls himself 'the Decider'." She adds, however, that "to decide is to kill off all possibilities but one." A good innovational thinker is always exploring the many other possibilities.

All of us work through problems in ways of which we're unaware, she says. Researchers in the late 1960s discovered that humans are born with the capacity to approach challenges in four primary ways: analytically, procedurally, relationally (or collaboratively) and innovatively. At the end of adolescence, however, the brain shuts down half of that capacity, preserving only those modes of thought that have seemed most valuable during the first decade or so of life.

The current emphasis on standardized testing highlights analysis and procedure, meaning that few of us inherently use our innovative and collaborative modes of thought. "This breaks the major rule in the American belief system – that anyone can do anything," explains M. J. Ryan, author of the 2006 book This Year I Will... and Ms. Markova's business partner. "That's a lie that we have perpetuated, and it fosters commonness. Knowing what you're good at and doing even more of it creates excellence." This is where developing new habits comes in.

## 四、完整文章翻译
习惯是个有趣的东西。我们不假思索地按照习惯行事，将大脑设定为自动驾驶模式，放松地沉浸在熟悉的常规所带来的无意识舒适感中。19世纪，威廉·华兹华斯曾说：“不是选择，而是习惯支配着那些不善于思考的大众。”在日新月异的21世纪，就连“习惯”这个词本身都带有负面含义。

因此，将习惯与创造力和创新放在同一语境下讨论，似乎显得自相矛盾。但大脑研究人员发现，当我们有意识地培养新习惯时，我们会在大脑中生成平行的神经通路，甚至生成全新的脑细胞，这些通路和细胞能将我们的思维列车引向全新的、富有创新性的轨道。

我们不应将自己视为无法改变的习惯性生物，相反，我们可以通过有意识地培养新习惯来主导自身的改变。事实上，我们尝试的新事物越多——我们走出舒适区的次数越多——我们就会变得越有内在创造力，无论是在工作中还是在个人生活中。

但不必费心去根除旧习惯；一旦那些程序化的惯例在大脑中根深蒂固，它们就会永远留在那里。相反，我们刻意培养的新习惯会生成平行的神经通路，这些通路可以绕过那些旧的神经通道。

《开放的思维》一书的作者道娜·马尔科娃说：“创新所需的首要条件是对好奇的痴迷。”“但我们反而被教导要‘做决定’，就像我们的总统自称‘决策者’一样。”然而，她补充道：“做决定就是扼杀除了一种可能性之外的所有其他可能性。”一个优秀的创新型思考者总是在探索众多其他的可能性。

她说，我们所有人都在用自己意识不到的方式解决问题。20世纪60年代末的研究人员发现，人类天生具有用四种主要方式应对挑战的能力：分析型、程序型、关系型（或协作型）和创新型。然而，在青春期结束时，大脑会关闭一半的这种能力，只保留在人生最初十年左右看起来最有价值的那些思维模式。

当前对标准化考试的强调突出了分析能力和程序能力，这意味着我们中很少有人会本能地运用创新和协作的思维模式。2006年出版的《今年我要……》一书的作者、同时也是马尔科娃女士商业伙伴的M·J·赖恩解释说：“这打破了美国信仰体系中的一条主要准则——任何人都能做成任何事。”“这是一个我们一直延续的谎言，它滋生了平庸。了解自己的长处并将其发挥到极致，才能成就卓越。”这正是培养新习惯的用武之地。
