# 2010text1

## 一、文章中英文主旨

### 中文主旨
本文围绕英文报纸行业的发展变迁展开，核心指出：在过去25年间，英文报纸的艺术报道在报道范围与内容严肃性上，发生了不可逆转的持续衰退。文章通过追忆20世纪初至二战前夕报纸艺术评论的黄金时代，以知名音乐评论家内维尔·卡达斯的人生与作品境遇为例，揭示了如今高质量艺术评论被大众遗忘、走向式微的现状，并分析了其难以实现复兴的深层原因。

### 英文主旨
This article focuses on the changes in English-language newspapers, pointing out that the most far-reaching change over the past quarter-century is the inexorable decline in the scope and seriousness of their arts coverage. By recalling the golden age of arts criticism in newspapers from the turn of the 20th century to the eve of World War II, and taking the famous music critic Neville Cardus as an example, it reveals the current situation that high-quality arts criticism has been forgotten by the public and fallen into decline, and analyzes the deep reasons why it is difficult to revive.

## 二、逐句精读解析（分段落）
### 第一段

#### 原句
Of all the changes that have taken place in English-language newspapers during the past quarter-century, perhaps the most far-reaching has been the inexorable decline in the scope and seriousness of their arts coverage.
#### 中文翻译
在过去25年里，英文报纸发生的所有变化中，影响最为深远的，或许是其艺术报道的范围与严肃性出现了不可阻挡的衰退。
#### 常考词汇&短语
- `take place` 发生；举行（考研高频固定搭配）
- `quarter-century` n. 四分之一个世纪，25年
- `far-reaching` adj. 影响深远的；广泛的
- `inexorable` adj. 不可阻挡的；无法改变的；不容变更的
- `decline` n./v. 衰退；下降；谢绝（考研核心词）
- `scope` n. 范围；视野；机会
- `coverage` n. 新闻报道；覆盖范围（考研高频词，媒体场景核心义）
#### 长难句结构分析
本句为复合句，主干为**the most far-reaching (change) has been the inexorable decline**。
1. 句首`Of all the changes...`为范围状语，限定主句描述的变化所属的范围；
2. `that have taken place...during the past quarter-century`是定语从句，修饰先行词`changes`，说明这些变化发生的场景和时间；
3. 主句中`the most far-reaching`后省略了前文出现的`change`，避免重复；
4. 句末`in the scope and seriousness of their arts coverage`是介词短语作后置定语，修饰`decline`，明确衰退的具体维度。

## 三、完整原文
① Of all the changes that have taken place in English-language newspapers during the past quarter-century, perhaps the most far-reaching has been the inexorable decline in the scope and seriousness of their arts coverage.

② It is difficult to the point of impossibility for the average reader under the age of forty to imagine a time when high-quality arts criticism could be found in most big-city newspapers. Yet a considerable number of the most significant collections of criticism published in the 20th century consisted in large part of newspaper reviews. To read such books today is to marvel at the fact that their learned contents were once deemed suitable for publication in general-circulation dailies.

③ We are even farther removed from the unfocused newspaper reviews published in England between the turn of the 20th century and the eve of World War II, at a time when newsprint was dirt-cheap and stylish arts criticism was considered an ornament to the publications in which it appeared. In those far-off days, it was taken for granted that the critics of major papers would write in detail and at length about the events they covered. Theirs was a serious business, and even those reviewers who wore their learning lightly, like George Bernard Shaw and Ernest Newman, could be trusted to know what they were about. These men believed in journalism as a calling, and were proud to be published in the daily press. “So few authors have brains enough or literary gift enough to keep their own end up in journalism,” Newman wrote, “that I am tempted to define ‘journalism’ as ‘a term of contempt applied by writers who are not read to writers who are’.”

④ Unfortunately, these critics are virtually forgotten. Neville Cardus, who wrote for the Manchester Guardian from 1917 until shortly before his death in 1975, is now known solely as a writer of essays on the game of cricket. During his lifetime, though, he was also one of England’s foremost classical-music critics, and a stylist so widely admired that his Autobiography (1947) became a best-seller. He was knighted in 1967, the first music critic to be so honored. Yet only one of his books is now in print, and his vast body of writings on music is unknown save to specialists.

⑤ Is there any chance that Cardus’s criticism will enjoy a revival? The prospect seems remote. Journalistic tastes had changed long before his death, and postmodern readers have little use for the richly upholstered Vicwardian prose in which he specialized. Moreover, the amateur tradition in music criticism has been in headlong retreat.

## 四、完整译文
① 在过去25年里，英文报纸发生的所有变化中，影响最为深远的，或许是其艺术报道的范围与严肃性出现了不可阻挡的衰退。

② 对于40岁以下的普通读者而言，他们很难甚至无法想象，曾经有一个时代，在大多数大城市的报纸上都能读到高质量的艺术评论。然而，20世纪出版的大量最重要的评论文集，其内容大部分都来自报纸评论。如今再去读这类书籍，人们会惊叹于这样一个事实：这些广博精深的内容，当年竟然被认为适合在面向大众发行的日报上刊登。

③ 我们与20世纪初至二战前夕在英国刊登的主题宽泛的报纸评论之间，距离甚至更远了。在那个时代，新闻用纸极其便宜，格调高雅的艺术评论被视为其所刊登刊物的一大亮点。在那个遥远的年代，主流报纸的评论家们会详尽、充分地记述他们所报道的事件，这被视为是理所当然的事。他们的工作是一件严肃的事，即便是像萧伯纳和欧内斯特·纽曼那样不卖弄学识的评论家，人们也相信他们清楚自己在做什么。这些人把新闻业视为一种职业使命，并且为自己的文章能刊登在日报上感到自豪。“能拥有足够的才智或文学天赋在新闻行业站稳脚跟的作家寥寥无几，”纽曼写道，“以至于我不禁想把‘新闻业’定义为‘无人问津的作家用来给受读者欢迎的作家扣上的一个蔑称’。”

④ 遗憾的是，这些评论家如今几乎已经被人遗忘了。内维尔·卡达斯从1917年到1975年去世前不久，一直为《曼彻斯特卫报》撰稿，如今人们只知道他是一名撰写板球赛事文章的作家。然而在他的一生中，他也是英国最顶尖的古典音乐评论家之一，同时还是一位广受赞誉的文体家，其1947年出版的《自传》曾是畅销书。他于1967年被授予爵士头衔，是首位获此殊荣的音乐评论家。然而如今他的著作只有一本还在版，他大量的音乐评论作品，除了专业人士之外，无人知晓。

⑤ 卡达斯的评论还有机会再度流行吗？前景似乎十分渺茫。早在他去世之前，新闻业的审美取向就已经发生了改变，后现代的读者们几乎不接受他所擅长的那种辞藻华丽、维多利亚-爱德华时代风格的散文。此外，音乐评论中的业余传统也在迅猛地走向衰落。
