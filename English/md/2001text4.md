## 一、中英文主旨
### 中文主旨
本文探讨了全球正在经历的前所未有的并购浪潮，分析了其产生的原因、带来的影响以及引发的担忧，指出并购浪潮背后的动力与全球化进程的动力一致，总体上对消费者有利，但同时也强调必须对这一运动进行密切监管。

### 英文主旨
This article discusses the unprecedented wave of mergers and acquisitions sweeping the globe, analyzes its causes, impacts and concerns, points out that the forces behind the M&A wave are the same as those driving globalization, which is generally beneficial to consumers, but also emphasizes that close supervision over this movement is necessary.

## 三、完整原文与完整翻译
### 完整原文
The world is going through the biggest wave of mergers and acquisitions ever witnessed. The process sweeps from hyperactive America to Europe and reaches the emerging countries with unsurpassed might. Many in these countries are looking at this process and worrying: "Won't the wave of business concentration turn into an uncontrollable anti-competitive force?"

There's no question that the big are getting bigger and more powerful. Multinational corporations accounted for less than 20% of international trade in 1982. Today the figure is more than 25% and growing rapidly. International affiliates account for a fast-growing segment of production in economies that open up and welcome foreign investment. In Argentina, for instance, after the reforms of the early 1990s, multinationals went from 43% to almost 70% of the industrial production of the 200 largest firms. This phenomenon has created serious concerns over the role of smaller economic firms, of national businessmen and over the ultimate stability of the world economy.

I believe that the most important forces behind the massive M&A wave are the same that underlie the globalization process: falling transportation and communication costs, lower trade and investment barriers and enlarged markets that require enlarged operations capable of meeting customers' demands. All these are beneficial, not detrimental, to consumers. As productivity grows, the world's wealth increases.

Examples of benefits or costs of the current concentration wave are scanty. Yet it is hard to imagine that the merger of a few oil firms today could re-create the same threats to competition that were feared nearly a century ago in the U.S., when the Standard Oil trust was broken up. The mergers of telecom companies, such as WorldCom, hardly seem to bring higher prices for consumers or a reduction in the pace of technical progress. On the contrary, the price of communications is coming down fast. In cars, too, concentration is increasing-witness Daimler and Chrysler, Renault and Nissan-but it does not appear that consumers are being hurt.

Yet the fact remains that the merger movement must be watched. A few weeks ago, Alan Greenspan warned against the megamergers in the banking industry. Who is going to supervise, regulate and operate as lender of last resort with the gigantic banks that are being created? Won't multinationals shift production from one place to another when a nation gets too strict about infringements to fair competition? And should one country take upon itself the role of "defending competition" on issues that affect many other nations, as in the U.S. vs. Microsoft case?

### 完整翻译
世界正在经历一场有史以来规模最大的并购浪潮。这一浪潮从极度活跃的美国席卷到欧洲，并以无可匹敌的力量延伸至新兴国家。这些国家的许多人看着这一进程，忧心忡忡：“这种商业集中的浪潮会不会变成一股无法控制的反竞争力量？”

毫无疑问，大企业正在变得更大、更强。1982年，跨国公司在国际贸易中所占的份额还不到20%。如今这一数字已超过25%，并且还在迅速增长。在那些对外开放并欢迎外资的经济体中，国际分公司在生产中所占的份额正在快速增长。例如在阿根廷，在20世纪90年代初的改革之后，跨国公司在200家最大企业的工业生产中所占的比重从43%上升到了近70%。这一现象引发了人们对小型企业、民族企业家的作用以及世界经济最终稳定性的严重担忧。

我认为，推动这场大规模并购浪潮的最重要力量，与推动全球化进程的力量是相同的：运输和通讯成本的下降、贸易和投资壁垒的降低，以及市场规模的扩大——这要求企业扩大经营规模以满足消费者的需求。所有这些对消费者来说都是有益的，而不是有害的。随着生产力的提高，世界财富也在增加。

目前关于这股集中浪潮带来的益处或代价的实例还很少。然而，很难想象如今几家石油公司的合并会重现近一个世纪前美国所担忧的那种对竞争的威胁，当时标准石油托拉斯被拆分了。像世通公司这样的电信公司的合并，似乎并没有给消费者带来更高的价格，也没有减缓技术进步的步伐。相反，通讯价格正在迅速下降。汽车行业也出现了集中化趋势——看看戴姆勒与克莱斯勒、雷诺与日产的合并就知道了——但消费者似乎并没有受到损害。

然而事实仍然是，我们必须密切关注这场并购运动。几周前，艾伦·格林斯潘就曾警告过银行业的巨型合并。对于正在形成的这些巨型银行，谁来负责监督、规范并充当最后贷款人的角色呢？当一个国家对违反公平竞争的行为监管过于严格时，跨国公司难道不会把生产从一个地方转移到另一个地方吗？而且，在那些影响到许多其他国家的问题上，比如美国诉微软案，是否应该由一个国家独自承担“捍卫竞争”的角色呢？
