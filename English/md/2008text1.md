## 一、文章主旨
### 中文主旨
本文通过科学研究和真实案例说明，女性比男性更容易因压力患上抑郁和焦虑症。其核心原因有二：一是性激素差异导致女性在压力下会产生更多的应激化学物质；二是女性承受的压力总量更多，且多为长期、反复的家庭类慢性压力。文章最后强调，在压力损害健康和正常生活能力之前，及时疏导压力至关重要。

### 英文主旨
This article illustrates through scientific studies and a real-life example that women are more susceptible to depression and anxiety disorders caused by stress than men. The two core reasons are: first, sex hormone differences make females produce more stress-triggering chemicals under pressure; second, women bear more stress in total, and most of it is long-term, repeated chronic stress from family situations. Finally, it emphasizes that it is crucial to relieve stress timely before it damages health and the ability to function normally.

## 三、完整原文
While still catching up to men in some spheres of modern life, women appear to be way ahead in at least one undesirable category. “Women are particularly susceptible to developing depression and anxiety disorders in response to stress compared to men,” according to Dr. Yehuda, chief psychiatrist at New York’s Veteran’s Administration Hospital.

Studies of both animals and humans have shown that sex hormones somehow affect the stress response, causing females under stress to produce more of the trigger chemicals than do males under the same conditions. In several of the studies, when stressed-out female rats had their ovaries (the female reproductive organs) removed, their chemical responses became equal to those of the males.

Adding to a woman’s increased dose of stress chemicals, are her increased “opportunities” for stress. “It’s not necessarily that women don’t cope as well. It’s just that they have so much more to cope with,” says Dr. Yehuda. “Their capacity for tolerating stress may even be greater than men’s,” she observes. “It’s just that they’re dealing with so many more things that they become worn out from it more visibly and sooner.”

Dr. Yehuda notes another difference between the sexes. “I think that the kinds of things that women are exposed to tend to be in more of a chronic or repeated nature.” Men go to war and are exposed to combat stress. Men are exposed to more acts of random physical violence. The kinds of interpersonal violence that women are exposed to tend to be in domestic situations, by, unfortunately, parents or other family members, and they tend not to be one-shot deals. The wear-and-tear that comes from these longer relationships can be quite devastating.

Adeline Alvarez married at 18 and gave birth to a son, but was determined to finish college. “I struggled a lot to get the college degree. I was living in so much frustration that that was my escape, to go to school, and get ahead and do better.” Later, her marriage ended and she became a single mother. “It’s the hardest thing to take care of a teenager, have a job, pay the rent, pay the car payment, and pay the debt. I lived from paycheck to paycheck.”

Not everyone experiences the kinds of severe chronic stresses Alvarez describes. But most women today are coping with a lot of obligations, with few breaks, and feeling the strain. Alvarez’s experience demonstrates the importance of finding ways to diffuse stress before it threatens your health and your ability to function.

## 四、完整译文
尽管在现代生活的某些领域女性仍在追赶男性，但至少在一个令人不快的方面，女性似乎遥遥领先。纽约退伍军人管理局医院的首席精神病学家耶胡达博士表示：“与男性相比，女性面对压力时特别容易患上抑郁症和焦虑症。”

对动物和人类的研究均表明，性激素会以某种方式影响压力反应，导致处于压力下的雌性比同等条件下的雄性产生更多的触发应激反应的化学物质。在几项研究中，当压力过大的雌性大鼠被切除卵巢（雌性生殖器官）后，它们的化学反应变得与雄性大鼠相同。

女性体内应激化学物质的剂量本就更高，而她们承受压力的“机会”也更多。耶胡达博士说：“这未必是因为女性应对压力的能力不如男性，只是她们要应对的事情多得多。”她指出：“她们忍受压力的能力甚至可能比男性更强。只是她们要处理的事情太多了，以至于她们会更早、更明显地因此疲惫不堪。”

耶胡达博士指出了两性之间的另一个差异。“我认为女性所接触到的各类事情往往更具有长期性或反复性的特点。”男性会上战场，面临战斗带来的压力。男性会遭遇更多随机的身体暴力行为。不幸的是，女性所遭受的人际暴力往往发生在家庭环境中，施暴者是父母或其他家庭成员，而且这种暴力往往不是一次性的。这些长期关系带来的消耗和折磨可能会造成极大的破坏。

阿德琳·阿尔瓦雷斯18岁结婚并生下一个儿子，但她决心完成大学学业。“为了拿到大学学位，我付出了很多努力。我当时生活在极度的沮丧之中，上学、取得进步、做得更好成了我的解脱。”后来，她的婚姻破裂了，她成了一名单身母亲。“要照顾一个十几岁的孩子、有一份工作、付房租、还车贷、还债务，这是最艰难的事情。我过着靠工资勉强糊口的日子。”

并非所有人都会经历阿尔瓦雷斯所描述的那种严重的慢性压力。但如今大多数女性都在承担着诸多责任，几乎没有休息时间，并且感到身心俱疲。阿尔瓦雷斯的经历表明，在压力威胁到你的健康和正常生活能力之前，找到疏导压力的方法至关重要。
