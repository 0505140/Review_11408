## 2005年考研英语一阅读Text 1 学习报告

## 一、文章主旨
### 中文主旨
本文通过一项关于雌性棕色卷尾猴的行为研究，论证了**动物与人类一样拥有公平感**：当受到不公平待遇时，动物会产生愤慨情绪；同时提出，这种公平感在人类和卷尾猴中是独立进化而来，还是源自3500万年前的共同祖先，目前仍是未解之谜。

### 英文主旨
Based on a behavioral study of female brown capuchin monkeys, this passage demonstrates that **animals, like humans, possess a sense of fairness**: they will feel resentful when treated unfairly. It also points out that whether this sense of fairness evolved independently in humans and capuchins, or derived from their common ancestor 35 million years ago, remains an unanswered question so far.

## 二、逐句翻译+核心词汇短语+长难句分析
### 第一段
1. **原文**：Everybody loves a fat pay rise.
   **翻译**：人人都喜欢大幅加薪。
   **常考单词/短语**：
   - fat：adj. 丰厚的，大量的（熟词生义，考研高频）
   - pay rise：加薪（英式表达，美式常用pay raise）

2. **原文**：Yet pleasure at your own can vanish if you learn that a colleague has been given a bigger one.
   **翻译**：然而，当你得知同事的加薪幅度比你更大时，你自己的喜悦感可能会瞬间消失。
   **常考单词/短语**：
   - pleasure：n. 愉快，喜悦
   - vanish：vi. 消失，不复存在（考研高频，同义：disappear）
   - colleague：n. 同事，同僚

3. **原文**：Indeed, if he has a reputation for slacking, you might even be outraged.
   **翻译**：事实上，如果这个同事向来以懒散出名，你甚至会感到怒不可遏。
   **常考单词/短语**：
   - indeed：adv. 的确，事实上（表强调）
   - have a reputation for：因…而闻名（褒贬均可，此处表贬义）
   - slack：vi. 懈怠，偷懒（考研高频，名词slackness）
   - outraged：adj. 愤怒的，愤慨的（动词outrage，名词outrage）

4. **原文**：Such behaviour is regarded as “all too human”, with the underlying assumption that other animals would not be capable of this finely developed sense of grievance.
   **翻译**：这种行为被认为是“人之常情”，其潜在的假设是：其他动物不具备这种高度发达的不满意识。
   **常考单词/短语**：
   - be regarded as：被认为是…（考研高频，同义：be considered as / be seen as）
   - all too human：人之常情（考研固定表达）
   - underlying：adj. 潜在的，根本的（考研高频，同义：implicit）
   - assumption：n. 假设，假定（动词assume）
   - be capable of：能够…（同义：be able to）
   - finely developed：高度发达的
   - sense of grievance：不满情绪，委屈感（grievance：n. 不满，委屈，考研高频）
   **长难句分析**：
   <big>
   句子结构：主句 + 伴随状语 + 同位语从句
   - 主句：`Such behaviour is regarded as “all too human”`（主系表结构，被动语态）
   - 伴随状语：`with the underlying assumption that...`（with复合结构作状语，补充说明主句的背景）
   - 同位语从句：`that other animals would not be capable of this finely developed sense of grievance`（解释说明assumption的具体内容，that在从句中不充当成分）
   </big>

5. **原文**：But a study by Sarah Brosnan and Frans de Waal of Emory University in Atlanta, Georgia, which has just been published in Nature, suggests that it is all too monkey, as well.
   **翻译**：但佐治亚州亚特兰大埃默里大学的萨拉·布罗斯南和弗兰斯·德·瓦尔的一项研究（该研究刚发表在《自然》杂志上）表明，这种情况也是“猴之常情”。
   **常考单词/短语**：
   - study：n. 研究（考研高频，同义：research）
   - publish：vt. 发表，出版（考研高频，名词publication）
   - Nature：《自然》（世界顶级学术期刊，考研阅读常出现）
   - all too monkey：猴之常情（对应前文all too human）
   - as well：也，同样（考研高频，同义：too / also）
   **长难句分析**：
   <big>
   句子结构：主句 + 非限制性定语从句 + 宾语从句
   - 主句：`a study... suggests that...`（主谓宾结构，主语a study被多个后置定语修饰）
   - 后置定语：`by Sarah Brosnan and Frans de Waal of Emory University in Atlanta, Georgia`（修饰study，说明研究的作者和机构）
   - 非限制性定语从句：`which has just been published in Nature`（修饰study，补充说明研究的发表情况，which在从句中作主语）
   - 宾语从句：`that it is all too monkey, as well`（作suggests的宾语，it指代前文提到的“因不公平待遇产生不满”的行为）
   </big>

## 三、完整文章原文
Everybody loves a fat pay rise. Yet pleasure at your own can vanish if you learn that a colleague has been given a bigger one. Indeed, if he has a reputation for slacking, you might even be outraged. Such behaviour is regarded as “all too human”, with the underlying assumption that other animals would not be capable of this finely developed sense of grievance. But a study by Sarah Brosnan and Frans de Waal of Emory University in Atlanta, Georgia, which has just been published in Nature, suggests that it is all too monkey, as well.

The researchers studied the behaviour of female brown capuchin monkeys. They look cute. They are good-natured, co-operative creatures, and they share their food readily. Above all, like their female human counterparts, they tend to pay much closer attention to the value of “goods and services” than males.

Such characteristics make them perfect candidates for Dr. Brosnan’s and Dr. de Waal’s study. The researchers spent two years teaching their monkeys to exchange tokens for food. Normally, the monkeys were happy enough to exchange pieces of rock for slices of cucumber. However, when two monkeys were placed in separate but adjoining chambers, so that each could observe what the other was getting in return for its rock, their behaviour became markedly different.

In the world of capuchins grapes are luxury goods (and much preferable to cucumbers). So when one monkey was handed a grape in exchange for her token, the second was reluctant to hand hers over for a mere piece of cucumber. And if one received a grape without having to provide her token in exchange at all, the other either tossed her own token at the researcher or out of the chamber, or refused to accept the slice of cucumber. Indeed, the mere presence of a grape in the other chamber (without an actual monkey to eat it) was enough to induce resentment in a female capuchin.

The researchers suggest that capuchin monkeys, like humans, are guided by social emotions. In the wild, they are a co-operative, group-living species. Such co-operation is likely to be stable only when each animal feels it is not being cheated. Feelings of righteous indignation, it seems, are not the preserve of people alone. Refusing a lesser reward completely makes these feelings abundantly clear to other members of the group. However, whether such a sense of fairness evolved independently in capuchins and humans, or whether it stems from the common ancestor that the species had 35 million years ago, is, as yet, an unanswered question.

## 四、完整文章翻译
人人都喜欢大幅加薪。然而，当你得知同事的加薪幅度比你更大时，你自己的喜悦感可能会瞬间消失。事实上，如果这个同事向来以懒散出名，你甚至会感到怒不可遏。这种行为被认为是“人之常情”，其潜在的假设是：其他动物不具备这种高度发达的不满意识。但佐治亚州亚特兰大埃默里大学的萨拉·布罗斯南和弗兰斯·德·瓦尔的一项研究（该研究刚发表在《自然》杂志上）表明，这种情况也是“猴之常情”。

研究人员对雌性棕色卷尾猴的行为进行了研究。它们看起来很可爱。它们性情温和、乐于合作，并且会欣然分享食物。最重要的是，和人类女性一样，它们往往比雄性更关注“商品与服务”的价值。

这些特征使它们成为布罗斯南博士和德·瓦尔博士研究的完美实验对象。研究人员花了两年时间教这些猴子用代币换取食物。通常情况下，猴子们很乐意用石块换取黄瓜片。然而，当两只猴子被安置在分开但相邻的房间里，能够互相看到对方用石块换来了什么时，它们的行为就会出现显著不同。

在卷尾猴的世界里，葡萄是奢侈品（而且远比黄瓜更受欢迎）。因此，当一只猴子用代币换到一颗葡萄时，另一只猴子就不愿意用自己的代币只换一片黄瓜了。如果一只猴子根本不用交出代币就能得到一颗葡萄，另一只猴子要么会把自己的代币扔向研究人员、扔出房间，要么会拒绝接受黄瓜片。事实上，仅仅是另一个房间里出现一颗葡萄（哪怕没有猴子去吃它），就足以引起雌性卷尾猴的不满。

研究人员认为，卷尾猴和人类一样，受社会情感的支配。在野外，它们是合作的群居物种。这种合作只有在每只动物都觉得自己没有被欺骗时，才有可能保持稳定。看来，义愤填膺的情绪并非人类所独有。拒绝接受更低的奖励，会向群体中的其他成员清晰地传达这种情绪。然而，这种公平感是在卷尾猴和人类身上独立进化而来，还是源自这两个物种3500万年前的共同祖先，至今仍是一个未解之谜。
