## （2006年Text 1）

### 一、文章主旨

**英文主旨**：This text argues that American society is a powerful homogenizing machine that successfully assimilates immigrants into its common culture, as evidenced by immigrants' mastery of English, high home ownership rates, and intermarriage, and concludes that today's American social environment is not dark or deteriorating despite some divisive issues.
**中文主旨**：本文指出美国社会是一台强大的同化机器，能成功将移民融入其大众文化中，通过移民对英语的掌握、高住房拥有率以及异族通婚等数据证明了这一点，并得出结论：尽管存在一些分裂性问题，当今美国的社会环境并未呈现黑暗、恶化的态势。

## 三、文章原文
In spite of "endless talk of difference," American society is an amazing machine for homogenizing people. There is "the democratizing uniformity of dress and discourse, and the casualness and absence of deference" characteristic of popular culture. People are absorbed into "a culture of consumption" launched by the 19th-century department stores that offered "vast arrays of goods in an elegant atmosphere." Instead of intimate shops catering to a knowledgeable elite" these were stores "anyone could enter, regardless of class or background. This turned shopping into a public and democratic act." The mass media, advertising and sports are other forces for homogenization.

Immigrants are quickly fitting into this common culture, which may not be altogether elevating but is hardly poisonous. Writing for the National Immigration Forum, Gregory Rodriguez reports that today's immigration is neither at unprecedented levels nor resistant to assimilation. In 1998 immigrants were 9.8 percent of the population; in 1900, 13.6 percent. In the 10 years prior to 1990, 3.1 immigrants arrived for every 1,000 residents; in the 10 years prior to 1890, 9.2 for every 1,000. Now, consider three indices of assimilation — language, home ownership and intermarriage.

The 1990 Census revealed that "a majority of immigrants from each of the fifteen most common countries of origin spoke English 'well' or 'very well' after ten years of residence." The children of immigrants tend to be bilingual and proficient in English. "By the third generation, the original language is lost in the majority of immigrant families." Hence the description of America as a "graveyard" for languages. By 1996 foreign-born immigrants who had arrived before 1970 had a home ownership rate of 75.6 percent, higher than the 69.8 percent rate among native-born Americans.

Foreign-born Asians and Hispanics "have higher rates of intermarriage than do U.S.-born whites and blacks." By the third generation, one third of Hispanic women are married to non-Hispanics, and 41 percent of Asian-American women are married to non-Asians.

Rodriguez notes that children in remote villages around the world are fans of superstars like Arnold Schwarzenegger and Garth Brooks, yet "some Americans fear that immigrants living within the United States remain somehow immune to the nation's assimilative power."

Are there divisive issues and pockets of seething anger in America? Indeed. It is big enough to have a bit of everything. But particularly when viewed against America's turbulent past, today's social indices hardly suggest a dark and deteriorating social environment.

## 四、文章完整翻译
尽管人们“无休止地谈论差异”，美国社会仍是一台令人惊叹的同化人的机器。大众文化具有“民主化的着装与话语统一，以及随意、不拘礼节”的特征。人们被19世纪百货商店所开创的“消费文化”所吸引，这些百货商店“在优雅的环境中提供琳琅满目的商品”。这些商店并非迎合知识精英的私密小店，而是“无论阶层或背景，任何人都可以进入”的场所。这将购物转变为一种公共、民主的行为。大众媒体、广告和体育是促成同化的其他力量。

移民正快速融入这种大众文化，这种文化或许并非完全高尚，但绝无危害。格雷戈里·罗德里格斯为国家移民论坛撰文时指出，如今的移民既未达到前所未有的规模，也不抗拒同化。1998年，移民占总人口的9.8%；1900年，这一比例为13.6%。1990年之前的10年间，每1000名居民中有3.1名移民迁入；1890年之前的10年间，每1000名居民中有9.2名移民迁入。现在，我们来考察同化的三个指标——语言、住房拥有率和异族通婚。

1990年的人口普查显示，“来自15个最常见移民来源国的大多数移民，在居住10年后，英语说得‘好’或‘非常好’”。移民的子女往往会说两种语言，并且精通英语。“到了第三代，大多数移民家庭的母语都消失了。”因此，美国被描述为语言的“坟墓”。到1996年，1970年之前迁入的外国出生移民的住房拥有率为75.6%，高于美国本土出生者69.8%的住房拥有率。

外国出生的亚裔和西班牙裔“异族通婚率高于美国本土出生的白人和黑人”。到了第三代，三分之一的西班牙裔女性嫁给了非西班牙裔，41%的亚裔美国女性嫁给了非亚裔。

罗德里格斯指出，世界各地偏远村庄的孩子都是阿诺德·施瓦辛格、加斯·布鲁克斯等超级明星的粉丝，然而“一些美国人担心，居住在美国的移民在某种程度上对这个国家的同化力免疫”。

美国是否存在分裂性问题和小范围的强烈不满？确实存在。美国足够大，包容万象。但尤其是与美国动荡的过去相比，如今的社会指标几乎不表明社会环境处于黑暗、恶化的状态。
