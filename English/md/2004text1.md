# 2004text1

## 一、文章主旨
### 中文主旨
本文以律师甘特·雷德蒙通过求职网站的“个人搜索代理”成功求职的案例引入，介绍了网络求职搜索代理的功能与作用，分析了该工具的优缺点与正确使用策略，讲解了求职网站设计搜索代理的商业逻辑，最后指出搜索代理对在职人群同样具备实用价值。

### 英文主旨

Starting with the case of lawyer Gant Redmon who successfully found a job via the "personal search agent" on a job-hunting website, this article introduces the functions of online job search agents, analyzes their advantages, disadvantages and proper using strategies, explains the business logic for job websites to design such agents, and finally points out that search agents are also valuable for employed people.

## 三、完整原文
Hunting for a job late last year, lawyer Gant Redmon stumbled across CareerBuilder, a job database on the Internet. He searched it with no success but was attracted by the site’s “personal search agent”. It’s an interactive feature that lets visitors key in job criteria such as location, title, and salary, then E-mails them when a matching position is posted in the database. Redmon chose the keywords legal, intellectual property and Washington, D.C. Three weeks later, he got his first notification of an opening. “I struck gold,” says Redmon, who E-mailed his resume to the employer and won a position as in-house counsel for a company.

With thousands of career-related sites on the Internet, finding promising openings can be time-consuming and inefficient. Search agents reduce the need for repeated visits to the databases. But although a search agent worked for Redmon, career experts see drawbacks. Narrowing your criteria, for example, may work against you: “Every time you answer a question you eliminate a possibility,” says one expert.

For any job search, you should start with a narrow concept — what you think you want to do — then broaden it. “None of these programs do that,” says another expert. “There’s no career counseling implicit in all of this.” Instead, the best strategy is to use the agent as a kind of tip service to keep abreast of jobs in a particular database; when you get E-mail, consider it a reminder to check the database again. “I would not rely on agents for finding everything that is added to a database that might interest me,” says the author of a job-searching guide.

Some sites design their agents to tempt job hunters to return. When CareerSite’s agent sends out messages to those who have signed up for its service, for example, it includes only three potential jobs — those it considers the best matches. There may be more matches in the database; job hunters will have to visit the site again to find them — and they do. “On the day after we send our messages, we see a sharp increase in our traffic,” says Seth Peets, vice president of marketing for CareerSite.

Even those who aren’t hunting for jobs may find search agents worthwhile. Some use them to keep a close watch on the demand for their line of work or gather information on compensation to arm themselves when negotiating for a raise. Although happily employed, Redmon maintains his agent at CareerBuilder. “You always keep your eyes open,” he says. Working with a personal search agent means having another set of eyes looking out for you.

## 四、完整译文
去年年末，律师甘特·雷德蒙在找工作时，偶然发现了互联网上的求职数据库网站“凯业必达（CareerBuilder）”。他在网站里搜索了一番毫无收获，却被网站的“个人搜索代理”功能吸引了。这是一项互动功能：访客可以输入工作地点、职位、薪资等求职要求，当数据库中发布了匹配的职位时，功能就会给访客发送邮件。雷德蒙选择了“法律”“知识产权”和“华盛顿特区”作为关键词。三周后，他收到了第一份职位空缺通知。“我挖到宝了，”雷德蒙说。他将简历通过邮件发给了雇主，最终成功获得了一家公司内部法务顾问的职位。

互联网上有数千个求职相关网站，找到有发展前景的职位空缺既耗时又低效。搜索代理减少了人们反复访问求职数据库的需求。但尽管搜索代理帮雷德蒙找到了工作，职业专家仍发现了它的弊端。比如，缩小你的筛选条件反而可能对你不利。一位专家表示：“你每回答一个筛选问题，就排除了一种可能性。”

无论找什么工作，你都应该先从一个狭窄的定位起步——也就是你自认想做的事——之后再拓宽求职范围。“这些程序都做不到这一点，”另一位专家说道。“这整套功能里完全没有内置职业咨询服务。”相反，最佳策略是把搜索代理当作一种提示服务，用来及时跟进特定数据库里的职位动态；当你收到邮件时，把它当成一个提醒，再去数据库里查看一遍。一位求职指南的作者表示：“我不会依赖搜索代理，去查找数据库里新增的所有我可能感兴趣的内容。”

一些网站会将搜索代理设计成能吸引求职者回访的模式。比如，当职业网站（CareerSite）的搜索代理向注册服务的用户发送消息时，只会包含三个潜在职位——也就是它认为匹配度最高的岗位。数据库里可能还有更多匹配的职位；求职者必须再次访问网站才能找到它们——而他们确实会回访。“在我们发送消息的第二天，网站的访问量就会出现大幅增长，”职业网站的营销副总裁塞思·皮茨说道。

即便那些不找工作的人，也会发现搜索代理很有价值。有些人用搜索代理密切关注自己所在行业的人才需求，或者收集薪酬信息，以便在协商加薪时更有底气。尽管已经拥有满意的工作，雷德蒙依然保留着他在凯业必达网站的搜索代理。“人要时刻留心机会，”他说。使用个人搜索代理，就相当于多了一双眼睛帮你留意机会。
