## 一、文章中英文主旨
### 中文主旨
本文围绕金融危机背景下银行业与会计准则制定机构的矛盾展开，指出银行公开为自身困境自责，私下却将矛头对准会计准则制定者，抱怨准则迫使他们报告巨额亏损；银行的游说已初见成效，导致会计准则制定机构的独立性受到损害。作者强调，独立的准则制定者是资本市场健康运行的核心，银行必须正视并处理亏损才能让体系重回正轨，而准则制定者向批评者妥协只会招致更多的让步压力。

### 英文主旨
This article focuses on the conflict between the banking industry and accounting standard-setters against the backdrop of the financial crisis. It points out that banks publicly blame themselves for their troubles, yet privately target accounting standard-setters, complaining that the rules force them to report enormous losses. Banks' lobbying has already worked, undermining the independence of accounting standard-setting bodies. The author emphasizes that independent standard-setters are the core of the sound operation of capital markets. Only when banks recognize and deal with losses can the banking system get back on track, and standard-setters' compromise to critics will only invite more pressure for further concessions.

## 二、逐句精读解析
### 段落1
#### 原句：Bankers have been blaming themselves for their troubles in public.
- 中文翻译：银行家们在公开场合一直为自身遭遇的困境自我归咎。
- 常考词汇&短语：
  - `blame sb for sth`：因某事指责/归咎于某人
  - `in public`：公开地，当众
  - `trouble`：n. 困境，麻烦

#### 原句：Behind the scenes, they have been taking aim at someone else: the accounting standard-setters.
- 中文翻译：在私下里，他们却一直将矛头对准了另一个群体：会计准则制定者。
- 常考词汇&短语：
  - `behind the scenes`：在幕后，私下里
  - `take aim at`：瞄准；将矛头对准，抨击
  - `accounting standard-setter`：会计准则制定者
  - `accounting`：n. 会计，会计学

#### 原句：Their rules, moan the banks, have forced them to report enormous losses, and it’s just not fair.
- 中文翻译：银行抱怨称，这些准则迫使他们报告巨额亏损，而这实在有失公允。
- 常考词汇&短语：
  - `moan`：v. 抱怨，发牢骚；呻吟
  - `force sb to do sth`：迫使某人做某事
  - `report`：v. 报告，披露
  - `enormous`：adj. 巨大的，庞大的
  - `loss`：n. 亏损，损失
  - `fair`：adj. 公平的，公允的
- 长难句结构分析：
  本句为倒装结构，正常语序为`The banks moan (that) their rules have forced them to report enormous losses, and it’s just not fair`。
  1. 句子主干为`and`连接的两个并列分句：
     - 分句1：`their rules have forced them to report enormous losses`（宾语从句，作`moan`的宾语）
     - 分句2：`it’s just not fair`
  2. `moan the banks`为插入语倒装，起到强调银行抱怨情绪的作用，突出其不满的态度。

#### 原句：These rules say they must value some assets at the price a third party would pay, not the price managers and regulators would like them to fetch.
- 中文翻译：这些准则规定，他们必须以第三方愿意支付的价格来评估部分资产的价值，而非按照管理者和监管机构期望这些资产能够卖出的价格来估值。
- 常考词汇&短语：
  - `value`：v. 估值，估价；重视
  - `asset`：n. 资产
  - `at the price of`：以……的价格
  - `third party`：第三方
  - `regulator`：n. 监管机构，监管者
  - `fetch`：v. 卖得，售得；取来
- 长难句结构分析：
  1. 句子主干：`These rules say [宾语从句]`
  2. 宾语从句主干：`they must value some assets at the price A, not the price B`，通过`not`形成两个估值价格的强对比
  3. 两个`price`后均接省略关系代词`that`的定语从句：
     - 定语从句1：`a third party would pay`，修饰第一个`price`，完整形式为`the price that a third party would pay`
     - 定语从句2：`managers and regulators would like them to fetch`，修饰第二个`price`，完整形式为`the price that managers and regulators would like them to fetch`

### 段落2
#### 原句：Unfortunately, banks’ lobbying now seems to be working.
- 中文翻译：不幸的是，银行的游说如今似乎已经产生了效果。
- 常考词汇&短语：
  - `unfortunately`：adv. 不幸的是，令人遗憾的是
  - `lobbying`：n. 游说（行为）
  - `work`：v. 起作用，奏效，产生效果

#### 原句：The details may be unknowable, but the independence of standard-setters, essential to the proper functioning of capital markets, is being compromised.
- 中文翻译：具体细节或许无从得知，但对资本市场的正常运转至关重要的准则制定者的独立性，正受到损害。
- 常考词汇&短语：
  - `unknowable`：adj. 无从得知的，不可知的
  - `independence`：n. 独立性
  - `essential to`：对……至关重要的
  - `proper functioning`：正常运转，正常运作
  - `capital market`：资本市场
  - `compromise`：v. 损害，危及；妥协，让步
- 长难句结构分析：
  1. 本句为`but`连接的并列转折句，分句1为`The details may be unknowable`，分句2为核心长难句
  2. 分句2主干：`the independence of standard-setters is being compromised`，采用现在进行时的被动语态，强调独立性正在被持续损害
  3. 插入语`essential to the proper functioning of capital markets`为形容词短语作后置定语，修饰`the independence of standard-setters`，相当于`which is essential to...`，补充说明准则制定者独立性的核心意义。

#### 原句：And, unless banks carry toxic assets at prices that attract buyers, reviving the banking system will be difficult.
- 中文翻译：而且，除非银行以能够吸引买家的价格计量有毒资产，否则复苏银行体系将会困难重重。
- 常考词汇&短语：
  - `unless`：conj. 除非，如果不
  - `carry`：v. （会计术语）计量，入账；携带
  - `toxic assets`：有毒资产
  - `attract`：v. 吸引
  - `revive`：v. 使复苏，振兴
  - `banking system`：银行体系
- 长难句结构分析：
  1. 句子主干：`reviving the banking system will be difficult`，动名词短语`reviving the banking system`作句子主语
  2. 句首为`unless`引导的条件状语从句，相当于`if not`，从句主干为`banks carry toxic assets at prices`
  3. 定语从句`that attract buyers`修饰先行词`prices`，明确价格需具备的属性。

### 段落3
#### 原句：After a bruising encounter with Congress, America’s Financial Accounting Standards Board (FASB) rushed through rule changes.
- 中文翻译：在与国会发生了一场激烈的交锋之后，美国财务会计准则委员会（FASB）仓促通过了准则修订方案。
- 常考词汇&短语：
  - `bruising`：adj. 十分激烈的；殊死的
  - `encounter with`：与……的交锋/冲突/相遇
  - `Congress`：n. （美国）国会
  - `Financial Accounting Standards Board (FASB)`：财务会计准则委员会（专有名词）
  - `rush through`：仓促通过，匆忙完成
  - `rule change`：准则修订，规则变更

#### 原句：These gave banks more freedom to use models to value illiquid assets and more flexibility in recognizing losses on long-term assets in their income statements.
- 中文翻译：这些修订方案赋予了银行更多的自由，使其能够使用模型来评估非流动性资产的价值，同时也让银行在损益表中确认长期资产的损失时拥有了更大的灵活性。
- 常考词汇&短语：
  - `freedom`：n. 自由
  - `model`：n. 模型，模式
  - `illiquid assets`：非流动性资产
  - `flexibility`：n. 灵活性
  - `recognize`：v. （会计术语）确认；识别，认出
  - `long-term assets`：长期资产
  - `income statement`：损益表，利润表（会计专有名词）
- 长难句结构分析：
  1. 句子主干：`These gave banks more freedom and more flexibility`，为双宾语结构，`give sb sth`，`banks`是间接宾语，`more freedom`和`more flexibility`是并列的直接宾语
  2. 后置定语1：`to use models to value illiquid assets`，不定式短语修饰`freedom`，说明自由的具体内容
  3. 后置定语2：`in recognizing losses on long-term assets in their income statements`，介词短语修饰`flexibility`，说明灵活性的适用场景。

#### 原句：Bob Herz, the FASB’s chairman, cried out against those who “question our motives.”
- 中文翻译：美国财务会计准则委员会主席鲍勃·赫茨对那些“质疑我们动机”的人表示了强烈抗议。
- 常考词汇&短语：
  - `chairman`：n. 主席，董事长
  - `cry out against`：强烈抗议，大声反对
  - `question`：v. 质疑，怀疑；提问
  - `motive`：n. 动机，目的
- 长难句结构分析：
  1. 句子主干：`Bob Herz cried out against those`
  2. 插入语`the FASB’s chairman`为同位语，补充说明Bob Herz的身份
  3. 定语从句`who “question our motives.”`修饰先行词`those`，指代发出质疑行为的人。

#### 原句：Yet bank shares rose and the changes enhance what one lobbying group politely calls “the use of judgment by management.”
- 中文翻译：然而银行股价应声上涨，并且这些修订方案也强化了一个游说团体委婉所称的“管理层判断力的运用”。
- 常考词汇&短语：
  - `yet`：conj. 然而，但是
  - `share`：n. 股票，股份
  - `enhance`：v. 增强，强化，提高
  - `politely`：adv. 委婉地，礼貌地
  - `judgment`：n. 判断，判断力
  - `management`：n. 管理层，管理
- 长难句结构分析：
  1. 本句为`and`连接的并列句，分句1：`bank shares rose`；分句2：`the changes enhance [宾语从句]`
  2. 宾语从句`what one lobbying group politely calls “the use of judgment by management.”`中，`what`在从句中作`call`的宾语，核心结构为`call A B`（把A称作B），`what`对应A，引号内的内容对应B。

  2. 同位语`a European commissioner`补充说明Charlie McCreevy的身份

     - 宾语从句1：`that it did “not live in a political vacuum” but “in the real world”`，使用`not...but...`（不是……而是……）结构，并列地点状语

### 段落5
#### 原句：It was banks that were on the wrong planet, with accounts that vastly overvalued assets.
- 中文翻译：脱离实际的正是银行，它们的账目对资产进行了大幅高估。
- 常考词汇&短语：
  - `on the wrong planet`：脱离实际，不切实际（固定习语）
  - `account`：n. 账目，账户
  - `vastly`：adv. 极大地，大幅地
  - `overvalue`：v. 高估，对……估价过高
- 长难句结构分析：
  1. 本句为强调句，核心结构为`It was + 被强调部分banks + that + 剩余内容`，专门强调主语`banks`
  2. 伴随状语`with accounts that vastly overvalued assets`补充说明银行的状态，其中`that vastly overvalued assets`是定语从句，修饰`accounts`。

#### 原句：Today they argue that market prices overstate losses, because they largely reflect the temporary illiquidity of markets, not the likely extent of bad debts.
- 中文翻译：如今，银行辩称，市场价格夸大了亏损，因为市场价格主要反映的是市场暂时的非流动性，而非坏账可能的规模。
- 常考词汇&短语：
  - `argue`：v. 辩称，主张；争论
  - `overstate`：v. 夸大，夸张
  - `largely`：adv. 主要地，在很大程度上
  - `reflect`：v. 反映，体现
  - `temporary`：adj. 暂时的，临时的
  - `illiquidity`：n. 非流动性
  - `extent`：n. 程度，规模，范围
  - `bad debts`：坏账，呆账
- 长难句结构分析：
  1. 句子主干：`they argue [宾语从句]`
  2. 宾语从句主干：`market prices overstate losses`
  3. 原因状语从句`because they largely reflect A, not B`，其中`they`指代`market prices`；A为`the temporary illiquidity of markets`，B为`the likely extent of bad debts`，通过`not`形成对比，明确银行的核心观点。

#### 原句：The truth will not be known for years.
- 中文翻译：真相在数年内都无从知晓。
- 常考词汇&短语：
  - `truth`：n. 真相，事实

#### 原句：But banks’ shares trade below their book value, suggesting that investors are skeptical.
- 中文翻译：但银行的股票交易价格低于其账面价值，这表明投资者持怀疑态度。
- 常考词汇&短语：
  - `trade`：v. 交易，买卖
  - `book value`：账面价值（会计专有名词）
  - `suggest`：v. 表明，暗示；建议
  - `investor`：n. 投资者
  - `skeptical`：adj. 持怀疑态度的，不相信的
- 长难句结构分析：
  1. 句子主干：`banks’ shares trade below their book value`
  2. 现在分词短语`suggesting that investors are skeptical`作结果状语，指代前面整个句子的内容，相当于`which suggests that...`
  3. `that`引导宾语从句，作`suggesting`的宾语。

#### 原句：And dead markets partly reflect the paralysis of banks which will not sell assets for fear of booking losses, yet are reluctant to buy all those supposed bargains.
- 中文翻译：而市场的停滞部分也反映了银行的瘫痪状态：银行因害怕确认亏损而不愿出售资产，同时也不愿收购那些所谓的廉价资产。
- 常考词汇&短语：
  - `dead market`：停滞的市场，萧条的市场
  - `partly`：adv. 部分地，在一定程度上
  - `paralysis`：n. 瘫痪，停滞
  - `for fear of`：害怕，唯恐
  - `book losses`：（会计术语）确认亏损，账面亏损
  - `be reluctant to do`：不愿做某事
  - `supposed`：adj. 所谓的，据称的
  - `bargain`：n. 便宜货，廉价品
- 长难句结构分析：
  1. 句子主干：`dead markets partly reflect the paralysis of banks`
  2. 定语从句`which will not sell assets..., yet are reluctant to buy...`修饰先行词`banks`，`which`在从句中作主语
  3. 从句包含两个由`yet`连接的并列谓语：`will not sell...` 和 `are reluctant to buy...`
  4. 原因状语`for fear of booking losses`修饰`will not sell assets`，说明银行不愿出售资产的核心原因。

## 三、完整原文
① Bankers have been blaming themselves for their troubles in public. Behind the scenes, they have been taking aim at someone else: the accounting standard-setters. Their rules, moan the banks, have forced them to report enormous losses, and it’s just not fair. These rules say they must value some assets at the price a third party would pay, not the price managers and regulators would like them to fetch.

② Unfortunately, banks’ lobbying now seems to be working. The details may be unknowable, but the independence of standard-setters, essential to the proper functioning of capital markets, is being compromised. And, unless banks carry toxic assets at prices that attract buyers, reviving the banking system will be difficult.

③ After a bruising encounter with Congress, America’s Financial Accounting Standards Board (FASB) rushed through rule changes. These gave banks more freedom to use models to value illiquid assets and more flexibility in recognizing losses on long-term assets in their income statements. Bob Herz, the FASB’s chairman, cried out against those who “question our motives.” Yet bank shares rose and the changes enhance what one lobbying group politely calls “the use of judgment by management.”

④ European ministers instantly demanded that the International Accounting Standards Board (IASB) do likewise. The IASB says it does not want to act without overall planning, but the pressure to fold when it completes its reconstruction of rules later this year is strong. Charlie McCreevy, a European commissioner, warned the IASB that it did “not live in a political vacuum” but “in the real world” and that Europe could yet develop different rules.

⑤ It was banks that were on the wrong planet, with accounts that vastly overvalued assets. Today they argue that market prices overstate losses, because they largely reflect the temporary illiquidity of markets, not the likely extent of bad debts. The truth will not be known for years. But banks’ shares trade below their book value, suggesting that investors are skeptical. And dead markets partly reflect the paralysis of banks which will not sell assets for fear of booking losses, yet are reluctant to buy all those supposed bargains.

⑥ To get the system working again, losses must be recognized and dealt with. America’s new plan to buy up toxic assets will not work unless banks mark assets to levels which buyers find attractive. Successful markets require independent and even combative standard-setters. The FASB and IASB have been exactly that, cleaning up rules on stock options and pensions, for example, against hostility from special interests. But by giving in to critics now they are inviting pressure to make more concessions.

## 四、完整译文
① 银行家们在公开场合一直为自身遭遇的困境自我归咎。在私下里，他们却一直将矛头对准了另一个群体：会计准则制定者。银行抱怨称，这些准则迫使他们报告巨额亏损，而这实在有失公允。这些准则规定，他们必须以第三方愿意支付的价格来评估部分资产的价值，而非按照管理者和监管机构期望这些资产能够卖出的价格来估值。

② 不幸的是，银行的游说如今似乎已经产生了效果。具体细节或许无从得知，但对资本市场的正常运转至关重要的准则制定者的独立性，正受到损害。而且，除非银行以能够吸引买家的价格计量有毒资产，否则复苏银行体系将会困难重重。

③ 在与国会发生了一场激烈的交锋之后，美国财务会计准则委员会（FASB）仓促通过了准则修订方案。这些修订方案赋予了银行更多的自由，使其能够使用模型来评估非流动性资产的价值，同时也让银行在损益表中确认长期资产的损失时拥有了更大的灵活性。美国财务会计准则委员会主席鲍勃·赫茨对那些“质疑我们动机”的人表示了强烈抗议。然而银行股价应声上涨，并且这些修订方案也强化了一个游说团体委婉所称的“管理层判断力的运用”。

④ 欧洲各国部长随即要求国际会计准则委员会（IASB）也采取同样的做法。国际会计准则委员会称，其不想在没有总体规划的情况下采取行动，但在今年晚些时候完成准则重构时，其做出让步的压力十分巨大。欧洲委员会委员查理·麦克里维警告国际会计准则委员会，它并非“生活在政治真空中”，而是身处“现实世界”，并且欧洲迟早可能制定出不同的准则。

⑤ 脱离实际的正是银行，它们的账目对资产进行了大幅高估。如今，银行辩称，市场价格夸大了亏损，因为市场价格主要反映的是市场暂时的非流动性，而非坏账可能的规模。真相在数年内都无从知晓。但银行的股票交易价格低于其账面价值，这表明投资者持怀疑态度。而市场的停滞部分也反映了银行的瘫痪状态：银行因害怕确认亏损而不愿出售资产，同时也不愿收购那些所谓的廉价资产。

⑥ 要让银行体系重新运转，就必须确认并处理亏损。美国收购有毒资产的新计划只有在银行将资产定价在买家认为有吸引力的水平时才会奏效。成熟的市场需要独立的、甚至是有战斗力的准则制定者。美国财务会计准则委员会和国际会计准则委员会此前正是这样的机构，例如，它们曾顶着特殊利益集团的敌意，完善了股票期权和养老金相关的准则。但如今向批评者妥协，它们将招致做出更多让步的压力。
