## 一、文章中英文主旨
### 中文主旨
本文以2006年世界杯优秀足球运动员更易在年初出生的反常现象为切入点，列举了对该现象的几种民间猜测，进而通过佛罗里达州立大学心理学教授安德斯·埃里克森的系列研究，驳斥了“先天天赋决定卓越成就”的固有认知，最终提出全文核心结论：各个领域的顶尖高手几乎都是后天练就的，而非天生的，刻意练习才是提升能力的核心关键。

### 英文主旨

This passage starts with the abnormal phenomenon that elite soccer players in the 2006 World Cup are more likely to be born in the earlier months of the year. It lists several folk guesses about this phenomenon, then through a series of studies by Anders Ericsson, a psychology professor at Florida State University, refutes the inherent cognition that innate talent determines outstanding achievement. Finally, it puts forward the core conclusion of the full text: top performers in various fields are almost always made, not born, and deliberate practice is the core key to improving ability.

## 三、完整原文
①If you were to examine the birth certificates of every soccer player in 2006’s World Cup tournament, you would most likely find a noteworthy quirk: elite soccer players are more likely to have been born in the earlier months of the year than in the later months. If you then examined the European national youth teams that feed the World Cup and professional ranks, you would find this strange phenomenon to be even more pronounced.

②What might account for this strange phenomenon? Here are a few guesses: a) certain astrological signs confer superior soccer skills; b) winter-born babies tend to have higher oxygen capacity, which increases soccer stamina; c) soccer-mad parents are more likely to conceive children in springtime, at the annual peak of soccer mania; d) none of the above.

③Anders Ericsson, a 58-year-old psychology professor at Florida State University, says he believes strongly in “none of the above.” Ericsson grew up in Sweden, and studied nuclear engineering until he realized he would have more opportunity to conduct his own research if he switched to psychology. His first experiment, nearly 30 years ago, involved memory: training a person to hear and then repeat a random series of numbers. “With the first subject, after about 20 hours of training, his digit span had risen from 7 to 20,” Ericsson recalls. “He kept improving, and after about 200 hours of training he had risen to over 80 numbers.”

④This success, coupled with later research showing that memory itself is not genetically determined, led Ericsson to conclude that the act of memorizing is more of a cognitive exercise than an intuitive one. In other words, whatever inborn differences two people may exhibit in their abilities to memorize, those differences are swamped by how well each person “encodes” the information. And the best way to learn how to encode information meaningfully, Ericsson determined, was a process known as deliberate practice. Deliberate practice entails more than simply repeating a task. Rather, it involves setting specific goals, obtaining immediate feedback and concentrating as much on technique as on outcome.

⑤Ericsson and his colleagues have thus taken to studying expert performers in a wide range of pursuits, including soccer. They gather all the data they can, not just performance statistics and biographical details but also the results of their own laboratory experiments with high achievers. Their work makes a rather startling assertion: the trait we commonly call talent is highly overrated. Or, put another way, expert performers – whether in memory or surgery, ballet or computer programming– are nearly always made, not born.

## 四、完整译文
①如果你去查看2006年世界杯足球赛所有参赛球员的出生证明，你极有可能会发现一个值得关注的奇特现象：优秀的足球运动员更有可能出生在一年的前几个月，而非后几个月。如果你再去查看为世界杯和职业球队输送人才的欧洲国家青年队，你会发现这一奇怪现象甚至更加明显。

②是什么导致了这一奇怪的现象呢？以下是几种猜测：a) 某些星座能赋予人更高超的足球技能；b) 冬季出生的婴儿往往有更高的血氧容量，这能提升足球运动所需的耐力；c) 痴迷足球的父母更有可能在春季（每年足球狂热的高峰期）受孕；d) 以上都不对。

③佛罗里达州立大学58岁的心理学教授安德斯·埃里克森表示，他坚信“以上都不对”。埃里克森在瑞典长大，曾学习核工程，直到他意识到，如果转攻心理学，他会有更多机会开展自己的研究。近30年前，他的首次实验与记忆力相关：训练一个人先听一组随机的数字，然后将其复述出来。埃里克森回忆道：“第一个实验对象在经过约20个小时的训练后，数字记忆广度从7提升到了20。他不断进步，在经过约200个小时的训练后，他能记住的数字超过了80个。”

④这次实验的成功，再加上后续表明记忆力本身并非由基因决定的研究，让埃里克森得出结论：记忆行为与其说是一种直觉活动，不如说是一种认知训练。换句话说，无论两个人在记忆能力上表现出怎样的先天差异，这些差异都会被每个人对信息的“编码”能力所掩盖。埃里克森断定，学习如何有意义地对信息进行编码的最佳方法，是一个被称为“刻意练习”的过程。刻意练习所需要的远不止是简单地重复一项任务。相反，它需要设定明确的目标、获取即时的反馈，并且既要关注结果，也要同等关注技巧。

⑤埃里克森和他的同事们由此开始研究包括足球领域在内的众多行业中的顶尖从业者。他们尽可能收集所有数据，不仅包括表现统计数据和生平细节，还包括他们自己对高成就者进行的实验室实验的结果。他们的研究得出了一个相当惊人的结论：我们通常称之为天赋的特质被严重高估了。或者换句话说，顶尖高手——无论是在记忆领域还是外科手术领域，无论是芭蕾舞还是计算机编程领域——几乎都是后天练就的，而非天生的。
